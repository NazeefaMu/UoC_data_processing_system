## Title: Czech drowns at Thalalla
## Section: Breaking News
## Date: 15 February 2015 12:18 pm
Czech drowns at Thalalla
	
A Czech national had drowned, while bathing at the Gandara Thalalla Sea this morning, the Police said.
The victim, identified as 46-year-old Velle Vency, had come to Sri Lanka on vacation with his family.
While staying at a hotel in Mirissa, they had gone to Thalalla to enjoy a sea bath, the Police said.
While bathing he had been washed away.
He was brought ashore with the help of the residents and was taken to the Matara Hospital, where he was pronounced dead.

## Title: Rajiva threatens to resign
## Section: Breaking News
## Date: 15 February 2015 06:00 pm
Rajiva threatens to resign
	
Professor Rajiva Wijesinghe said today that he had written to President, Maithripala Sirisena saying he would resign his post as State Minister of Higher Education if he were not allowed to take decisions relating to his duties.
Professor Wijesinghe told Daily Mirror that he had considered several options and one was to resign his post.
He had written to the president on Wednesday about the situation that would arise if decisions were taken without consulting his ministry.
He had said that decisions taken like that could not be approved by him and cited the removal of the University Grants Commission chairperson as an example.
However, according to sources close to Prime Minister Ranil Wickremesinghe, the chairperson had been asked to resign in keeping with the policy of the new government -- which was to ask all officials attached to state institutions to vacate their posts.
In response to his letter, Professor Wijesinghe had been told that the president had informed Highways, Investment Promotion and Higher Education Minister Kabir Hashim -- though Prof. Wijesinghe had not been informed.
Prof. Wijesinghe had requested the president to make him a cabinet minister in order to solve the problem.?
?
Professor Wijesinghe said he had tried to contact the prime minister yesterday but he had been away. However he was confident that the prime minister would take up the matter today and come up with a satisfactory solution.

## Title: Will not leave SLFP, supporters-MR

Will not leave SLFP, supporters-MR
	
Former President Mahinda Rajapaksa in a letter to SLFP General Secretary Anura PriyadharsanaYapa had assured that despite criticism, insults and sidelining, he would not leave the party or the party supporters in any circumstance.
Informing his inability to attend the SLFP?s Executive Committee meeting at Battaramaulla on Saturday, the former President congratulated the new office bearers of the SLFP.He had said in the letter that he could not attend the meeting due to his other commitments.

?I firmly believed that the newly elected President of the SLFP, MaithripalaSirisena and other office bearers will take the responsibility of protecting the party as well as its members.
I can bear the continuous threat, insults and sidelining me from both inside and outside, as a person, who able to stand against the world?s ruthless terrorists as well as the international conspiracies,? he stated.

## Title: Woman arrested scribbling on the Sigiriya frescos
## Section: Breaking News
## Date: 15 February 2015 08:56 am
Woman arrested scribbling on the Sigiriya frescos
	
A 27-year-old woman had been arrested for damaging the Sigiriya frescos yesterday, the Police said.
They woman, a resident of Batticaloa, had visited Sigiriya along with her friends had scribbled her name on one of the frescoes using her hairpin.
Police said the Archaeology Department officials had handed over the woman to the Sigiriya Police, while she was writing her names on the frescos.
Police said the woman would be produced before the Dambulla Magistrate Court today.(DS)
