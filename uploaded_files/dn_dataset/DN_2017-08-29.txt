## Title: Man found unconscious in Kochchikade dies in hospital
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Local
## Date: Tuesday, August 29, 2017 - 13:45
Man found unconscious in Kochchikade dies in hospital

An unidentified man, who was found lying unconscious near a shop in Dalulotuwa Junction, Kochchikade breathed his last at the Negombo Hospital on August 24
He was admitted to the Hospital on July 23.
The unidentified man is about 55 year old, 5.6ft with grey hair.
The body is lying at the Negombo hospital morgue.
Police seek public assistance to identify the body

## Supertitle: 
## Section: Local
## Date: Tuesday, August 29, 2017 - 15:00
Principal sentenced to eight years imprisonment for accepting bribe

A former principal who accepted a bribe of Rs. 25,000 to admit a child to a school was sentenced to an eight-year imprisonment by the Colombo High Court today

## Title: Rohitha Abeygunawardena corruption case re-fixed for trial on Oct. 12
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Law & Order
## Date: Tuesday, August 29, 2017 - 16:15
Rohitha Abeygunawardena corruption case re-fixed for trial on Oct. 12

A corruption case filed against former Minister Kalutara District MP Rohitha Abeygunawardena for obtaining assets worth Rs.41.1 million was re-fixed for trial on October 12 by the Colombo High Court, today.
Meanwhile, High Court Judge Sampath Wijeratne permitted Rohitha Abeygunawardena to go abroad between September 24 and October 10.
 At an earlier occasion, the High Court overruled a preliminary objection raised by former Minister against the Bribery Commission's decision to amend the charges.
 The Director General of the Commission to Investigate Allegations of Bribery or Corruption had filed an indictment in the Colombo High Court on two counts against former Minister Rohitha Abeygunawardena for the unlawful acquisition of assets estimated at Rs.41.1 million.
 In his indictment, the Director General of the Bribery Commission alleged that the former Minister had committed this offence by way of undeclared assets in between 2004 to 2006.
 The Director General alleged that the former minister had unlawfully made 66 monitory transactions, constructed a house and purchased a jeep in contravention to the section 23(a) of the Bribery Act.
The Director General further alleged that former Minister Abeygunawardena owned these properties-deems under the provision of the act to be property acquired by bribery.

## Title: Sri Lanka Cricket selection committee resigns
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Local
## Date: Tuesday, August 29, 2017 - 17:15
Sri Lanka Cricket selection committee resigns

The Sri Lanka Cricket (SLC) National Selection Committee headed by former cricketer Sanath Jayasuriya has tendered their resignation today.
The committee comprising of former cricketers Romesh Kaluvitharana, Ranjith Madurusinghe and Eric Upashantha was appointed by Sports Minister Dayasiri Jayasekera.
The resignations were tendered following the consistent defeats faced by the Sri Lankan Cricket team in the series against Zimbabwe and India.
