## Title: 13 accused in 2008 youth abduction case released on bail
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Law & Order
## Date: Friday, January 24, 2020 - 13:52
13 accused in 2008 youth abduction case released on bail
The Permanent High Court at Bar ordered to release on bail the 13 accused including the Former Naval Media Spokesperson Commodore DKP Dassanayake, in connection with the abduction and disappearance of 11 youth between 2008 and 2009.Accordingly, each accused has been released on a cash bail of Rs. 500,000 and two sureties of Rs. 2.5 million each.

## Title: Committee appointed to probe misconduct at bus depots
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Local
## Date: Friday, January 24, 2020 - 15:28
Committee appointed to probe misconduct at bus depots
A special investigative committee has been appointed to probe the various malpractices that had allegedly taken place at Sri Lanka Transport Board and its island wide bus depots for many years.The Committee will also investigate allegations of misconduct leveled against the Sri Lanka Transportation Board by the public.Passenger Transport Management, Power and Energy Minister Mahinda Amaraweera has advised Ministry Secretary Gamini Senevirathne to appoint the committee and handover the final Committee report to him within two weeks following an extensive investigation of all allegations and complaints received against SLTB and bus depots.Accordingly, Secretary to the Ministry has appointed a four-member committee led by Passenger Transport Management Ministry Additional Secretary of Planning Tilakaratne Banda. The other members are Senior Assistant Secretary Naomi Jayawardena, Legal Officer Priyanthi Gunasekera, and Internal Audit Officer V.K.H. Kolitha.

## Title: Death fast 1,132 ft underground.....
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Local
## Date: Friday, January 24, 2020 - 01:09
Death fast 1,132 ft underground.....
The first unto death launched by employees attached to the Dodangaskanda Kahatagaha Graphite (Mines) Lanka Company in support of several demands continued for the third day yesterday.
The employees who are conducting the fast inside the mine, almost 1,132 fleet below ground level, have threatened to go down to 2,000 feet unless their demands were met.
The employees said their main demand was for the management to rectify their salary anomalies. They said they had not received any salary increments since 2012, and they are paid salaries on two different scales.

## Title: SriLankan Airlines takes action with regard to Coronavirus outbreak
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Local
## Date: Friday, January 24, 2020 - 15:47
SriLankan Airlines takes action with regard to Coronavirus outbreak
SriLankan Airlines has implemented a series of precautionary measures to mitigate the risks from the current coronavirus epidemic in the Far East, with the safety and well-being of its valued passengers and staff being of paramount importance, the airline in a statement said.SriLankans cabin crew and other relevant staff have been briefed on actions to be taken on board and at airports, especially those in the Far East.The airline is also in contact with relevant aviation and health authorities who are involved in monitoring the situation. This includes Ministry of Health and the authorities at Colombos Bandaranaike International Airport, the statement said.
