## Title: Death toll rises at Meethotamulla: Ten dead, several injured in garbage dump collapse
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Local
## Date: Saturday, April 15, 2017 - 10:00
Death toll rises at Meethotamulla: Ten dead, several injured in garbage dump collapse

The death toll at the Meethotamulla garbage dump has risen to 10.
Many more are feared buried under the rubble.
A section of the garbage dump at Meethotamulla yesterday collapsed on residents burying as many as 40 houses.
President Maithripala Sirisena ordered the tri-forces immediately to engage in rescue operations.
Rescue operations are continuing.
The injured have been taken to the Colombo National Hospital.

## Title: 'No more garbage at Meethotamulla': Harsha de Silva
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Local
## Date: Saturday, April 15, 2017 - 17:00
'No more garbage at Meethotamulla': Harsha de Silva
Deputy Foreign Affairs Minister Harsha de Silva today said no more garbage would be dumped at Meethotamulla.
He said two alternate locations had been identified instead.
Expressing condolences to the families that lost loved ones, the Deputy Foreign Affairs Minister said the state would bear the cost of the funerals.
The Deputy Foreign Affairs Minister said furthermore, a special coordination committee was appointed by the President this morning to manage the situation at Meethotamulla.
On the orders of the President and PM a coordination committee to manage the situation was established this morning.Present were Ministers Sagala Ratnayake, Susil Premjayanth, Patali Champika Ranawaka, A H M Fowzie, Mohan Lal Grero and myself along with MPs S M Marikar and Mujibur Rahman.Commander of the Army and IGP and all relevant government officials were also present.'
'Major General Sudantha Ranasinghe was put in charge of search and rescue where an area of about 150m by 75m is being carefully cleaned up. The military is using its trained men and equipment and is doing a thorough job to look for those who might be still trapped. The utility lines have been shutoff and safety concerns addressed for the personnel on the job, the Deputy Foreign Affairs Minister said.
He said Senior DIG Nandana Munasinghe has been placed in charge of public order.
The District Secretary of Colombo is overall in charge of all administrative coordination matters. Already close to a hundred people have been shifted to temporarily holding locations; schools and others will be shifted as well. PM has ordered the release all necessary funds from the Treasury to deal with all financial requirements, the Deputy Foreign Affairs Minister said.
He added that it was ironic that agreements to begin waste-to-energy projects with refuse at the Meethotamulla dump was signed only a few weeks before.
The Deputy Foreign Affairs Minister also said the payments had been made to the families at risk of landslides, but they had not moved out as encouraged. He said the families had been advised to leave as recently as two days ago.

## Title: Shoppers warned against A (H1N1), other viral infections
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Local
## Date: Thursday, April 13, 2017 - 01:00
Shoppers warned against A (H1N1), other viral infections
Health experts warn the public to take precautions against the spreading Influenza A (H1N1) and other viral infections due to the hot weather, while engaging in New Year shopping and visiting crowded places during the festive season. The public can protect themselves by covering the mouth and nose with a handkerchief or tissue when coughing and sneezing and refraining from touching the eyes, nose or mouth, avoiding spitting in public, washing hands regularly with soap and water or clean hands with alcohol-based hand rubs, disposing used tissues/handkerchiefs carefully and washing used handkerchiefs carefully with germ killer soap/liquid etc before re-using.
Meanwhile, Epidemiology Unit sources said that a total of 30,486 dengue patients and around 60 deaths had been reported countrywide during the past three months. According to sources,the highest number of dengue patients(60,906) had been reported from the Colombo district. The second highest number of dengue patients(3,853) had been reported from the Gampaha district.
The Trincomalee district recorded 2,820 dengue patients and the Jaffna district 1,884 patients.
Prompt medical treatment should be sought from a state hospital or a qualified doctor if anyone suffer from fever for more than two days. No drug other than Paracetamol should be used for fever to avoid complications such as Dengue Hemorrhagic Fever, Dengue Shock Syndrome etc, sources added.
