## Title: Joint Opposition off to Geneva
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Political
## Date: Tuesday, March 29, 2016 - 16:30
Joint Opposition off to Geneva
The Joint Opposition will submit a complaint to the Geneva based Inter-Parliamentary Union about alleged harassment, intimidation and injustices they face both within parliament and outside.

A joint opposition team comprising of Dullus Alahapperuma, Udaya Gammanpila and Bandula Gunewardene today left for Geneva to meet the IPU hierarchy.
Speaking to the media on the eve of his departure MP Alhapperuma said the joint opposition members will meet the IPU Secretary General in Geneva on Thursday.

Alahapperuma said that the delegation had decided to visit Geneva after the IPU positively responded to a letter from the joint opposition for a meeting.
The Matara district parliamentarian said that the team will complain to the IPU regarding privilege issues faced by the joint opposition within parliament and also about harassment outside

Alahapperuma said the joint opposition will explain in detail about harassment faced by them which were the result of a political witch-hunt.
Alahhaperuma said the team will make the IPU aware about issues such as the joint opposition not being given adequate time at parliament sessions, not being given parliamentary responsibilities and attempts at intimidation and harassment using law enforcement authorities.

## Title: Sports meet of Mahamaya primary school
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Sports
## Date: Tuesday, March 29, 2016 - 01:00
Sports meet of Mahamaya primary school

The sports meet of Mahamaya girls school primary section was held under the patronage of Dr. Mrs. Shyama Arambepola, a child psychiatric specialist in Kandy hospital. The meet was held at the Bogambara Stadium on the 11th of March 2016.
The principal of the school Indra Vithanarachchi received the chief guest as well as the other distinguished guests. Primary School head Shiranthi Weerasekara, assistant principal delivered the vote of thanks.
