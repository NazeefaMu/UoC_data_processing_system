## Title: [Cabinet Decisions]-07.11.2015
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Political
## Date: Saturday, November 7, 2015 - 01:00

[Cabinet Decisions]-07.11.2015

 
Decisions taken by the Cabinet of Ministers at the meeting held on November 05, 2015
01. Financial Assistance from the Asian Development Bank (ADB) for the implementation of the Greater Colombo Water and Waste Water Management Improvement Programme - Tranche 3 (Document No - 05)
The Colombo Water and Waste Water Management Improvement programme is being implemented in three phases under financial facilities by the ADB and government funds to uplift the water supply efficiency in Greater Colombo. Total investments of the Phase III of the project is US$ 168.46 million, while US$ 40.46 million by the government. The proposal made by Prime Minister Ranil Wickremesinghe in his capacity as National Policies and Economic Affairs Minister to obtain the balance of US$ 128 million form the ADB as concessionary loan, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
 
02. Gampaha, Attanagalle and Minuwangoda Integrated Water Supply Project (Document No - 08)
The proposal made by Minister of City Planning and Water Supply Rauf Hakeem, to implement the above project with a cost of US$ 229.5 million and Rs. 3,910 million benefitting the people of Gampaha, Attanagalle and Minuwangoda areas, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
 
03. Amendment of the Fisheries (Regulating of Foreign Fishing Boats) Act No 59 of 1979 (Document No - 12)
Regulating, control and management of fishing and related activities by foreign fisheries boats in Sri Lankan waters is done according to the provisions made by Fisheries (Regulating of Foreign Fishing Boats) Act No 59 of 1979. However, according to changes in the field regionally and internationally, a new Act has to be introduced amending the relevant provisions. Accordingly, a proposal by Fisheries and Aquatic Resources Development Minister Mahinda Amaraweera, to amend the Fisheries (Regulating of Foreign Fishing Boats) Act, No. 59 of 1979, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
 
04. Domestic production of Smart Electric Metres to be introduced to Sri Lanka's electricity field (Document No - 13)
The electricity field in Sri Lanka had gained massive development in the recent past and has taken steps to provide approximately 100 percent coverage 24 hours continuously. As technology is rapidly developing, utilizing higher technological techniques in generation, distribution and usage, many benefit the national economy. Introduction of Smart Electric Meters is an urgent need. It helps the demand for electricity management and enhance awareness in the consumer in self-control in its use. Accordingly, the proposal made by Power and Renewable Energy Minister Ranjith Siyambalapitiya to commence a joint project between the Ceylon Electricity Board and the University of Peradeniya for development and introduction of Smart Electric Meters to the domestic market costing Rs. 49.2 million, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
 
05. Implementation of the National Programme for Food Production 2016 - 2018 (Document No - 14)
Cabinet approval had been granted to implement the above programme which is presented by the President on October 14, 2015, while the Ministry of Agriculture has prepared a national programme in fulfilling its objectives. It is proposed to implement the programme in two aspects, mainly related to 14 fields essential for the increase of food production and agricultural development. Accordingly, the proposal made by Agriculture Minister Duminda Dissanayake, to implement the programme in 2016-2018 and obtain required allocations, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
 
06. Assignment of ownership of state lands on a long term lease basis to the National Livestock Development Board (NLDB) and its farms being established (Document No - 19)
The NLDB is currently operating 13 farms on state land within the island for the development of the livestock industry. Ownership of the lands are not hitherto assigned to the NLDB. The joint proposal made by Rural Economic Affairs Minister P. Harrison and Lands Minister M.K.A.D.S. Gunawardena, , to assign the ownership of state lands on a long term lease basis to the NLDB and its farms which are being established, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
 
07. Awarding of contracts for buildings constructed at Universities of Kelaniya, Peradeniya and Postgraduate Institute of Medicine (Document No - 28)
The proposal made by University Education and Highways Minister Lakshman Kiriella, to award the following contracts as per recommendations by the Cabinet Appointed Procurement Committees, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
(i). Contract for the development of the Postgraduate Institute for Medicine - Rs. 1,412.4 million.
(ii). Contract for construction of a building complex at the Department of Industrial Management in the Science Faculty of the University of Kelaniya for facilitating the introduction of new graduate courses in the Information and Communication Technology field - Rs. 430.1 million.
(iii). Contract for the development of the Faculty of Agriculture of the University of Peradeniya - Rs. 342.3 million.
 
08. Implementation of the University of Ruhuna Township Development Project (Document No - 29)
The government has planned to develop a University Township centralizing the University of Ruhuna in Wellamadama in close proximity to the Matara town. Under this project it has been planned to implement two sub projects for development of common facilities, Ocean Aquarium and a Freshwater Aquarium and related infrastructure at a cost of Rs. 2,512.8 million.
 
Following programmes are proposed to be implemented under the first sub project:
* Determination and declaring of boundaries of the University Township.
* Construction of an irrigation canal connecting the Kumbalgama Lake and Dondra lagoon and construction of a walking track on one of its banks.
* Construction of a swimming pool and development of sports and recreation facilities within the University Township.
The proposal made by University Education and Highways Minister Lakshman Kiriella, to implement the first sub project of the University of Ruhuna Township Development Project at a cost of Rs. 556.6 million, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
 
09. The ASB assisted Greater Colombo Water and Waste Water Management Improvement Investment Programme - Ambatale Water Supply System Improvement and Energy Saving Project (Document No - 32)
The proposal made by City Planning and Water Supply Minister Rauf Hakeem, to award contracts for laying of pipelines from Ambatale to Elli House Reservoir, a length of 9km under the above project as recommended by the Cabinet Appointed Standing Procurement Committee, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
 
10. Supply of official uniforms and other clothing material for Sri Lanka Police (Document No - 33)
The proposal by Law and Order and Prison Reforms Minister Thilak Marapana, to obtain clothing material for the Sri Lanka Police for the year 2015 from local textile manufacturers as per the recommendations of the Local Textile Allocation Committee, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
 
11. Green Power Development and Energy Efficiency Improvement Investment Programme - Construction of Kalutara, Kesbewa and Old Anuradhapura Grid Substations and augmentation of New Anuradhapura Grid Substation (Document No - 34)
The proposal made by Power and Renewable Energy Minister Ranjith Siyambalapitiya, to award the above contract to the lowest bidder as recommended by the Cabinet Appointed Standing Procurement Committee, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
 
12. Green Power Development and Energy Efficiency Improvement Investment Programme - Construction of Kappalthurai Grid Substation and augmentation of Kerawalapitiya, Trincomalee and Katunayake Grid Substations (Document No - 35)
The proposal made by Power and Renewable Energy Minister Ranjith Siyambalapitiya, to award the above contract as recommended by the Cabinet Appointed Standing Procurement Committee, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
 
13. Financial Assistance from the International Fund for Agricultural Development (IFAD) for the implementation of the Smallholder Tea and Rubber Revitalization (STARR) Programme (Document No - 37)
The above programme had commenced by the government with financial assistance from the IFAD. It intends to assist the improvement of tea yields in 5,500 hectare in Matara, Galle, Ratnapura, Badulla and Kandy Districts. Moreover, it would assist in improving Rubber yields in 3,000 hectare in Moneragala and Ampara Districts. The total investment would be US$ 65.4 million and US$ 25.7 million, granted by the IFAD. The proposal made by Prime Minister Ranil Wickremesinghe in his capacity as the National Policies and Economic Affairs Minister, to enter into an agreement with the IFAD on the above purpose was, approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
 
14. Considering Higher National Diploma in Accountancy (HNDA) as an alternative qualification to B.Com degree (Document No - 38)
Students who have followed the HNDA course have faced difficulties in applying for public sector vacancies as P.A. Circular No. 46/90 issued in 1990 considering the HNDA course as an alternative qualification to B.Com degree, was nullified by the P.A. Circular No. 10/2014 last year. Diploma holders have been disappointed due to the private sector demand for job opportunities have also been reduced taking this as a precedent.
As about 23,500 students from among 142,000 students who qualify for University entrance are being admitted to state universities, many of them who have followed the commerce stream tend to join the HNDA course annually. Considering the above facts, the proposal made by University Education and Highways Minister Lakshman Kiriella, to re-enforce the P.A. Circular 46/90 with effect from 23-04-2014, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
 
15. Issuance of a stamp to commemorate the 60th anniversary of Sri Lanka - Thailand diplomatic relationships (Document No - 39)
Diplomatic relations between Thailand and Sri Lanka completes its 60th anniversary in 2015. As per a proposal made by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, the Postal Department of Sri Lanka and the Postal Institute of Thailand have taken steps to issue two stamps each, to mark the occasion.
The stamp is issued based on Buddhism, a common theme to both countries. The Postal Institute of Thailand has issued the stamp on November 02, 2015, in line with the official visit of the Sri Lankan President in Thailand and the Postal Department of Sri Lanka expects to issue the stamp on November 20, 2015. The proposal made by Posts, Postal Services and Muslim Religious Affairs Minister M.H.A. Haleem, to sign a MoU between the two countries on the above purpose, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
 
16. International Monetary Fund (IMF) / World Bank (WB) Annual Meeting held in Lima, Peru from October 8 - 11, 2015 (Document No - 41)
Finance Minister Ravi Karunanayake, has informed the Cabinet that a delegation presided by him had participated in the above annual meetings and had discussed the Global Economic Foresight with special attention on economic issues of developing countries. The minister said the main causes for issues faced by developing countries are slow growth of the production sector and political issues, while developing countries should apply suitable policies to face risks of financial instability, unemployment and revenue anomalies.
Furthermore, the Finance Minister had informed the Cabinet of Ministers that he had participated in many discussions with Finance Ministers of various countries that play an important role in our foreign trade and with senior officials of the IMF.
 
17. Technical Cooperation - Grant of Euro 7 million from the Government of Federal Republic of Germany (Document No - 42)
The Government of the Federal Republic has agreed to provide technical cooperation grant of Euro 7 million for the implementation of three projects, i.e., National Park and Buffer Zone Management in Wilpattu, Education for Social Cohesion and SME Sector Development. The proposal made by Finance Minister Ravi Karunanayake, to sign exchange notes between the two countries on the above purpose, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
