## Title: Colts, SLPA and NCC record victories
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Sports
## Date: Monday, January 11, 2016 - 01:00

Colts, SLPA and NCC record victories

The latest left arm sensation Prabath Jayasuriya bagged a match bag of 10 as Colts beat Badureliya SC by an innings and 46 runs at Surrey Village, Maggona. Batting first Colts piled 344 on the back of knocks of 86 and 65 from Hashan Dumindu and Sadeera Samarawickrema.
Colts Cricket Club : 344 in 89 overs (Sadeera Samarawickrama 65, Hashan Dumindu 86, Dulanjana Mendis 3/76, Chamath Gunasinghe 3/77)
Badureliya Sports Club : 134 in 45.5 overs (Nadeera Nawela 46, Prabath Jayasuriya 5/25, Dilruwan Perera 4/35 & F/O 164 (41.1) Roscoe Thattil 57, Prabath Jayasuriya 5/58, Dilruwan Perera 2/31) - COLTS WON BY AN INNINGS & 46 RUNS
Komasaru Spins Ports to a much needed Win
Thirty tow year old experienced left arm spinner Chanaka Komasaru took a match bag of 10 to secure a much needed win for SLPA.Coming into the game after a bashing from Tamil Union last week SLPA put up a much improved performance to restrict CCC for just 92 runs after they elected to bat first.
The CCC wicket favored the spinners as Pathiran and Sandakan also struggled the SLPA batsman from the outset. Batting woes continued for Ports, but Gihan Rupasinghe in a lone assault hit a 49 ball 83 to give Ports a vital 36 run lead in the first innings.Komasaru and Aravinda Premartne shared 7 wickets as the spinners back in the act bundling out CCC for 126 in the second innings. Chasing a target of 91 Ports achieved the feat in 14.2 overs.
Colombo CC : 92 in 30 overs (Sachith Pathirana 25, Chanaka Komasaru 6/29) and 126 in 32.1overs (Andy Solomons 40, Chanaka Komasaru 4/33, Aravinda Premarathne 3/21, Harsha Rajapaksha 2/13)
SLPA CC : 128 in 26.4 overs (Gihan Rupasinghe 83, Sachith Pathirana 5/32, Lakshan Sandakan 3/33 and 93/1 in 14.2 overs (Ashan Priyanjana 50) - SLPA CC WON BY 9 WICKETS
NCC record Innings Win
Chamikara Edirisinghe the frontline spinner for NCC this season picked up a match bag of 13 as SSC beat Army by an innings and nine runs. In the first innings Chamikara picked up 7/57 as Army was bowled out for 190.
NCC in reply were 5/96 when Jehan Mubarak and Anuk Fernando batted sensibly to steady the ship. Anuk Fernando scored a gritty 70 whilst Mubarak contributed with a fluent 78. NCC posted 228 gaining a slender lead of 38 runs.
SL Army SC : 190 in 56.5 overs (Asela Gunarathne 67*, Dushan Vimukthi 38, Shalika Karunanayake 30, Chamikara Edirisinghe 7/57) and 92 in 27 overs (Sanjika Ridma 37*, Chamikara Edirisinghe 5/38)
NCC : 228 in 90.2 overs (Jehan Mubarak 78, Anuk Fernando 79, Nuwan Liyanapathirana 2/35, Lakshan Madushanka 2/78, Seekkuge Prasanna 2/40) and 59/1 in 6.3 overs (Upul Tharanga 31*, Niroshan Dickwella 25*) - NCC WON BY 9 WICKETS
SSC clinch first innings points against Galle CC
Dasun Shanaka scored 108 whilst Thilina Kandamby, Kavindu Kulasekara and Harith Maduwantha contributed with half centuries to earn vital first innings points for SSC in their clash against Galle CC. Batting first Galle CC posted 376 as Chamara Silva top scored with 108 runs. Rumesh Buddhika and Nisal Randika continued their good form with the bat making scores of 85 and 69. Galle CC were restricted to 201 in their second innings at stumps on final day.
Galle CC : 376 in 80.4 overs (Nisal Randika 69, Rumesh Buddika 85, Chamara Silva 108*, Dasun Shanaka 6/69) and 201 in 64 overs (Chamara Silva 51, Suraj Randiv 33*, Tharindu Ratnayake 4/65, Binura Fernando 2/25)
SSC : 399 in 100 overs (Dasun Shanaka 108, Thilina Kandamby 82, Harith Maduwantha 62, Kavindu Kulasekara 56, TN Sampath 4/74, Suraj Randiv 3/109) - MATCH DRAWN
Ganegama's maiden Ton in vain as Saracens edge out Chilaw for first innings points
Akalanka Ganegama who is known for his fast bowling opened the batting for Chilaw and scored his first first class Ton, but his team fell short by 5 runs being all out for 400 losing vital first innings points for Saracens. Batting first Saracens posted 405/9 before declaration thanks to a 174 partnership by Alankara Asanka Silva and Tharindu Priyan. The latter scored 64 whilst AA Silva scored an unbeaten 102.
Saracens SC : 405/9declared in 131.2 overs (Alankara Asanka 102*, Rajith Priyan 64, Sangeeth Cooray 53, Lahiru Gamage 5/149, Maduka Liyanapathiranage 2/55)
Chilaw MCC : 400 in 129.4 overs (Akalanka Ganegama 133, Saliya Saman 60, Thikshila de Silva 38, Umesh Karunaratne 4/101, Akila Isanka 3/134)- MATCH DRAWN
Ragama in first innings win
Lahiru Milantha and Udara Jayasundara added 221 for the first wicket helping Ragama CC to overtake the score of 426 posted by Tamil Union. Batting first in form Tamil Union posted 426 on the back of half centuries from Dananjaya De Silva (91), Jeevan Mendis (82), Sithara Gimhan (76) and Manoj Sarathchandra (59).Ragama going into the second innings with a decent lead of 133 runs had the chance of attacking the Tamil Union batsman during the final session of play. However, Pabasara Waduge scored a solid unbeaten 63 as Tamil Union were 134/1 at stumps ending the match in a draw.
Tamil Union : 426 in 95.5 overs (Dhananjaya de Silva 91, Jeewan Mendis 82, Manoj Sarathchandra 59, Sithara Gimhan 76, Sanjaya Fernando 4/74, Amila Aponso 3/104) and 134/1 in 34 overs (Pabasara Waduge 63*, Tharanga Paranavithana 34*)
