## Title: Govt. paying Rs.140mn as rental for ministries
## Section: Breaking News
## Date: 24 March 2017 07:15 am

Govt. paying Rs.140mn as rental for ministries


	

The government pays nearly Rs 140 million monthly as rent for the offices where ministries are located, Parliament was informed yesterday.

Chief Government Whip Gayantha Karunatilake who was responding to an oral question raised by JVP MP Nalinda Jayatissa said the government paid monthly rent to 33 buildings where ministries are located.

Accordingly Rs 24 million is being paid for the premises of the ministry of agriculture while Rs. 14 million is being paid for the premises where the ministry of childcare and women's affairs is located , Rs 11 million for the premises of special empowerment ministry and Rs. 11.3 million is being paid for the ministry of Road Development premises.

It was also revealed that some ministries including the ministry of foreign affairs, public administration, National Policies and Economic Affairs and ministry Foreign Employment have rented more than two office buildings to carry out their tasks. (Yohan Perera and Ajith Siriwadana)
