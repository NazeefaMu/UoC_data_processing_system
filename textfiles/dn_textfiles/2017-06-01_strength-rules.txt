## Date: Thursday, June 1, 2017 - 01:00

Strength rules

He had represented Athletics and Rugby for his Alma Mater, Kingswood College, Kandy, in the inter schools championships. Then he went on to represent the Sri Lanka national weightlifting and powerlifting teams and won the Gold medals in the Asian and Commonwealth games. Incidentally he has a rare achievement of representing in four sports, weightlifting, powerlifting, athletics and rugby, in his sports carrier. Today we go ‘Off the Field’ with this sensational sports legend Ransilu Jayathilake.
Q: What made you enter the world of sports?
 
The influence of my father.
 
Q: Who has supported you the most?
 
My father, mother and my family.
 
Q: What do you do for fun?
 
Watching movies.
 
Q: What are the priorities in your life?
 
My family.
 
Q: What was your most unforgettable memory/incident?
 
Winning the Commonwealth Games Gold Medal
 
Q: What’s your favourite holiday destination?
 
Miami in the USA.
 
Q: What is the greatest accomplishment in your Life?
 
Getting qualified last year to the world powerlifting championship. I am the first Sri Lankan to qualify for the world powerlifting championship.
 
Q: Who do you sometimes compare yourself to?
 
I don’t compare myself to anyone but I do compare myself to my past and be better than what it was before.
 
Q: If you had one day left to live, what would you do?
 
Obviously going to the gym.
 
Q: What’s the best compliment you have received?
 
Current world champion and the strongest man, Blaine Summer, offering his powerlifting gear to me as a present which he wore to break the world record in the 2016 IPL world championship in USA.
 
Q: Are you happy with all your achievements?
 
I never get satisfy with my achievements.
 
Q: What’s your favourite movie?
 
Sunday Night Lights.
 
Q: Are you doing what you believe in, or are you settling for what you are doing?
 
Yes, I am doing what I believe in and enjoy what I do.
 
Q: How would you describe yourself in two words?
 
 
Go-getter
