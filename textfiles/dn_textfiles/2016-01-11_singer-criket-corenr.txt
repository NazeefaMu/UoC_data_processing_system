DSS secure first innings
win over Thurstan
Torin Pitigala's solid 87 with the bat followed by dominant bowling performances by Muditha Lakshan (4/40) and Anjana de Silva (3/34) immensely helped DS Senanayaka record a first innings win over much fancied Thurstan in their traditional fixture that ended in a tame draw at DSS ground recently.
St.Peter's College batsman Lakshina Rodrigo.
Meanwhile, Royal Colombo surged to an innings win over St.Anthonys, Kandy in one of their traditional fixtures played in Colombo in which Thiran Dhanapala scored a century for Royal.

Niduka's 166 saves Mahanama from humiliation
Niduka Welikala tried valiantly posting one of the best individual innings this season to ensure Mahanama would pass triple figures after following on with a first innings deficit of 224 and avoid innings defeat, but it was all in vain as Zahira needing just 96 to score in the fourth innings to pull off an outright win were home comfortably with 7 wickets to spare in the end.
Meanwhile, the traditional match between St.Servatius, Matara and Bandaranayaka, Gampaha played at Uyanwatta in Matara ended without producing any positive results at a time Servatius were just two wickets away from an outright win.

De Mazenod win first innings honours
Krishan Sanjula scoring an unbeaten century and Deshan Fernando a patient 82 in a tremendous 188-run partnership for the second wicket helped De Mazenod earn vital first innings points after they declared their innings at 232 for three in reply to Dharmaraja's first innings score of 192, in their Group A match concluded at De Mazenod Ground, Kandana in a tame draw.
Krishan's 117 scored from 130 balls had 9 fours and four sixes in it. Dharmaraja batting for the second time around with 40 runs behind on the first innings had made 115 for five at close on the final day.
Dharmaraja College : 192 in 79.4 overs (Gihan Vitharana 36, Ruchira Pupulewatta 43, Supun Vithanachchi 33, Akash Senarathna 3/29, Mithila Geeth 2/44) and 115-5 in 53 overs (Ruchira Pupulewatta 42*, Deshan Gunasinghe 24, Avishka Indrajith 2/00)

Gurukula, S.Thomas' and Trinity in big wins
Gurukula College, Kelaniya after going down on first innings in their three previous outings within their Group surged to their maiden win in the season beating Moratu Vidyalaya by an innings and 15 runs.
Gurukula all-rounder, Tharindu Tharaka completing an 8-wicket match bag and 52 runs with the bat became the best performer either with the bat or ball in this match. Meanwhile, in the four traditional matches concluded on Tuesday, S.Thomas' Mt.Lavinia and Trinity came out with highest outcomes to record an innings win over St.Benedict and an outright win against Dharmapala respectively whilst Maliyadewa and St.Sebastians Moratuwa won first innings honours in their matches, against Mahinda-Galle and St.Josephs Colombo respectively, that ended in high scoring draws.
