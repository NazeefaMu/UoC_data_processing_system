## Title: World Rubber Summit 2018  concludes
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Finance
## Date: Wednesday, May 9, 2018 - 01:00

World Rubber Summit 2018  concludes

The World Rubber Summit (WRS) 2018 organized by the International Rubber Study Group together with the Ministry of Plantation Industry began in Colombo concluded yesterday at Taj Samudra Hotel, Colombo.
Last several years, this summit was held in Singapore, and this is the first time in several years that it was held outside Sri Lanka. The theme of the World Rubber Summit 2018 was “Breaking barriers towards sustainable growth.”
WRS created a unique and exclusive opportunity for the global rubber industry to discuss the current challenges that the industry is facing and discuss the future opportunities. There were key discussions on current and future industry trends in rubber production and consumption at the event.
The keynote speech of the WRS-2018 was made by the Navin Dissanayake, Minister of Plantation Industries.
The International Rubber Study Group (IRSG) is an inter-governmental organization comprised of rubber producing and consuming stakeholders. IRSG currently has 36-member countries and 120 industry members. Republic of Cameroon, Cote d’Ivoire, The European Union (28 Member States), India, Japan, Nigeria, Russian Federation, Singapore and Sri Lanka are also among the member Governments.of2IRSG.The summit was a good platform to find new foreign markets and expand the existing markets for natural rubber and finished products of Sri Lanka.
According to the 2017 Annual report of the Central Bank, rubber exports brought in revenue of 38.9 million US Dollars.
The Annual Report shows that rubber production has increased by 5.1 percent to 83.1 million kilogrammes in 2017, from 79.1 million kilogrammes recorded in 2016.
There were also field visits to rubber manufacturing plants and rubber estates.
