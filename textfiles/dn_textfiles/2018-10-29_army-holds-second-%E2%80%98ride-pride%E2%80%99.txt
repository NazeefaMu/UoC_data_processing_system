## Title: Army holds second ‘Ride with Pride’
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Local
## Date: Monday, October 29, 2018 - 01:00

Army holds second ‘Ride with Pride’

The second installment of ‘Ride with Pride’, a health-oriented bicycle riding project organised by the army, was launched yesterday with the participation of over 200 cyclists at the Galle Face Green.
Army Commander Lieutenant General Mahesh Senanayake initiated the event and took part in the cycling tour as well.
Cyclists from the army, navy, air force and civil sector took part in the event, which was chaired by Army Sports Director-General Major General Anura Sudasinghe. ‘Ride with Pride’ was organised by the Army Sports Directorate, with assistance from the Army Cycling Committee headed by Brigadier Duminda Sirinaga.
Cyclists made their way along Galle Road to Kollupitiya, R.A. de Mel Mawatha, Moratuwa, Panadura and took a turn at the Walana junction to return to the Army Electrical and Mechanical Engineers’ Base Workshop at Katubedda, where the journey ended.
At the Galle Face Green, Lieutenant General Mahesh Senanayake was welcomed by Army Chief of Staff Major General Dampath Fernando.
