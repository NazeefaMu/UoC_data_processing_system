## Title: [Cabinet Decisions]-25.03.2016
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Political
## Date: Friday, March 25, 2016 - 01:00

[Cabinet Decisions]-25.03.2016

 
Companies Act to be amended
The proposal by Industry and Commerce Minister Rishad Bathiudeen to make with immediate effect amendments to the Companies Act, as advised by the Advisory Commission and the Auditor General, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
------
US-SL exchange of tax information
The proposal by Prime Minister Ranil Wickremesinghe, in his capacity as National Policies and Economic Affairs Minister, to enter into an agreement between the Internal Revenue Services of USA and the Department of Inland Revenue Sri Lanka, to mutually exchange tax information, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
Waste Management Project (Matara)
The proposal by President Maithripala Sirisena, in his capacity as Mahaweli Development and Environment Minister, to implement a waste water management project, with the cooperation of the private sector, in the Matara Municipal Council and Matara Pradeshiya Sabha areas was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
---------
Integrated Water Supply Projects Divineguma Development - Compensation and Gratuity payment
The proposal by Prime Minister Ranil Wickremesinghe, in his capacity as the National Policies and Economic Affairs Minister, to authorize the National Water Supply and Drainage Board to obtain the necessary funds through a local bank to finance the Polgahawela, Pothuhera, and Alawwa Integrated Water Supply Project; the Kundasale-Haragama Water Supply Project and Aluthgama, Mathugama and Agalawatta Integrated Water Supply Project, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
--------
Rail track from Kurunegala to Habarana via Dambulla
The proposal by Transport and Civil Aviation Minister Nimal Siripala de Silva to acquire lands and recruit staff to accelerate a project to construct eight km of rail track from Kurunegala to Habarana via Dambulla, with the help of the Republic of China, under the 'Let's Awaken Polonnaruwa' District Development Programme for 2016-2020, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
---------
Horana Town Improvement
The joint proposal by Social Empowerment and Welfare Minister S.B Dissanayake and Finance Minister Ravi Karunanayake to obtain funds to pay compensation and gratuity to officers who have served in the institutions which were combined to establish the Department of Divineguma Development, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
The proposal by Megapolis and Western Development Minister Patali Champika Ranawaka to implement the Second Stage of the Development Plan prepared by the Urban Development Authority for the Horana Urban Council Area - the construction of small scale shopping spaces, sanitary facilities, and improving access road , and also implementing the Horana Bus Stand Development Project - at a cost of Rs. 182 million - was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
---------
Social Welfare Centre (Mattakkuliya)
The proposal by Megapolis and Western Development Minister Patali Champika Ranawaka to establish a Social Welfare Centre at Mattakkuliya to uplift the lives of poor and the underprivileged people of Colombo North at a cost of Rs. 250 million, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
The proposed building will host healthcare, childcare, community welfare, e-channelling, library, gym, carom, chess and other facilities. Construction is to be completed within a year and ownership and management is to be assigned to the Colombo Municipal Council.
---------
Annual contribution to CAPSA
The proposal by Agriculture Minister Duminda Dissanayake to pay the annual subscription to the Centre for Alleviation of Poverty through Sustainable Agriculture ( CAPSA) was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers. CAPSA is a sub-institution governed by the UN's Economic and Social Commission with an objective to provide policy inducement for member countries, to uplift the living standard of the rural people and engage in development and research functions on agriculture. Sri Lanka has benefited greatly from its membership.
--------
Mediation Boards Act amendment
The proposal by Justice Minister Wijayadasa Rajapaksa to publish the Bill prepared in this regard in the gazette and to submit it in Parliament for approval, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers. The Cabinet had already granted approval to increase the limit of monetary value in respect of disputes to be referred to compulsory mediation from Rs. 250,000/- to Rs. 500,000-/.
-------
Land for Parliamentary Reforms, Mass Media Ministry
The proposal by Parliamentary Reforms and Mass Media Minister Gayantha Karunathilaka to purchase 40 perches of land for Rs. 31.5 million, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers, on the recommendation of the Ministerial Committee on Economic Management.
The Parliamentary Reforms Ministry is currently housed in a private building in Battaramulla at a rental cost of Rs. 630,000.
---------
Middle Income Housing and Infrastructure
The proposal by Housing and Construction Minister Sajith Premadasa, to establish a Programme Management Unit to efficiently implement a five year plan to provide houses for middle income wage earners in urban and suburban areas in all districts was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
--------
Contract for refined petroleum products
The proposal by Petroleum Resources Development Minister Chandima Weerakkody, to award a four term contract for the period of eight months from 01st March 2016, for purchasing of refined petroleum, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers
------
Relaunching Gam Udawa
The proposal by Housing and Construction Minister Sajith Premadasa to relaunch 'Gam Udawa', the Village Reawakening Programme, and the Gam Udawa Exhibition - held in line with the programme in several electorates - from this year on, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
The programme was initially launched in the 1980s but stopped in mid 1990s.
--------
Contract for Ariel Bundle Conductors
The proposal by Power and Renewable Energy Minister Ranjith Siyambalapitiya to award the above contract as recommended by the Cabinet Appointed Standing Procurement Committee, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
-------
Road work on Ja-Ela to Chilaw
The proposal by Prime Minister Ranil Wickremesinghe, in his capacity as the Minister of National Policies and Economic Affairs, to conduct loan negotiations with the International Development Agency and enter agreement to obtain a credit of 90.5 million special drawing rights (Approximately US$ 126.7 million) to work on the section of road from Ja-Ela to Chilaw of the A3 road under the Transport Connectivity and Asset Management Project, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
-----
ADB credit for SMEs
The Ministry of Finance entered into an agreement to obtain a loan of US$ 100 million from the Asian Development Bank to strengthen Small and Medium scale enterprises by granting them loan facilities.
Loans will be granted to launch an enterprise or to expand an existing enterprise subject to a maximum of Rs. 50 million with a repayment period of 10 years.
The proposal by Finance Minister Ravi Karunanayake to implement the loan scheme and enter into agreements with Participatory Financial Institutions, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
