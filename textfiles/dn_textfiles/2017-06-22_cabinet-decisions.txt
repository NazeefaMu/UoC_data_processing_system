## Title: Cabinet Decisions
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Political
## Date: Thursday, June 22, 2017 - 01:00

Cabinet Decisions

Improvement of theatres and ICUs in hospitals under Austrian loan scheme
The government of Austria has agreed to grant a concessionary loan of 9.58 million Euros for providing equipments including anesthetic equipment, endoscopy machines, theatres and mobile lighting systems for selected hospitals. Accordingly, the proposal made by National policies and Economic Affairs Minister Niroshan Perera, to enter in to relevant agreements in this regard, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
Development and improvement of the Institute of Engineering and Technology, Katunayake
Cabinet of Ministers has granted approval for awarding of contact for the above project on March 14. Government of Austria has agreed to provide a concessionary loan of 9.5 million Euros in this regard and the proposal made by National policies and Economic Affairs Minister Niroshan Perera, to hold negotiations and enter in to relevant agreements in this regard, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
Annual bus fare amendment - 2017
According to the powers vested in National Transport Commission, it has prepared annual bus fare revisions for year 2017. According to the proposal of the commission, the proposals made by Transport and Civil Aviation Minister Nimal Siripala de Silva to increase existing bus fares from 6.28 percent and to increase the minimum fare of Rs. 9.00 to Rs. 10.00, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
Conducting feasibility studies for the construction of Yatimahana Reservoir in Ma Oya basin and Vee Oya Reservoir in Kelani River basin
Above projects are to be implemented as a solution to water shortage for water supply schemes in dry spells by constructing Vee oya Reservoir with a capacity of 19 million cubic metres and Yatimahana Reservoir with a capacity of 12.7 million cubic metres. The proposal made by City Planning and Water Supply Minister Rauff Hakeem, to conduct feasibility studies in this regard and to assign the task to Mahaweli Consultancy Bureau (Pvt) Ltd, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
'Divi Udana' concessionary interest loan scheme programme - 2017
Steps have been taken to implement eight loan schemes, according to 2017 budget proposals, from March 31. The programme is implemented successfully through commercial banks, the Ministry of Finance and mass media has entered in to agreements with relevant banks and the public have been made aware of the programme through electronic and print media. The information presented by Finance and Mass Media Minister Mangala Samaraweera, was noted by the Cabinet of Ministers.
Establishment of a University College in Trincomalee District
Vocational Training Centres located in Trincomalee District only offers courses in NVQ 3 and 4 levels and students should go to University Colleges in Anuradhapura or Jaffna to follow courses in NVQ 5 or 6. Therefore, the proposal made by Skills, Development and Vocational Training Minister Chnadima Weerakkody, to establish a University College in Trincomalee District at a cost of Rs. 434 million, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
Lease out a land to H.V.A. farms (Pvt.) Ltd. on long-term basis for environment-friendly organic fruits and vegetable cultivation
The Proposal made by Land and Parliamentary Reforms Minister Gayantha Karunathilaka, to provide the above land of 69.9 hectares, which is located in Ralmaduwa Grama Niladhari Division in Vanatha Villuwa Divisional Secretary's Division in Puttalam District on the above purpose, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers
Sri Lanka Technological University
Above University was established with funds of Sri Lanka Telecom and It was recognized as a degree offering institute by the Ministry of Higher Education and Highway in 2017. This will provide degree courses in engineering for 2017 A/L students and it has entered into agreements with university of Lancaster in UK and Deakin University in Australia for exchange of students.
It will take further action to commence such programmes with Georgia Technological University in USA, Technological University in Texas, and Lincoln University in New Zealand. A Graduate Studies Unit has also been established under this University. Above information presented by of Telecommunication and Digital Infrastructure Minister Harin Fernando, was noted by the Cabinet of Ministers.
Construction of a water supply operation station for Horana Zone of BOI Sri Lanka
Horana Industrial Estate has created 2,500 employment opportunities by various projects. A sufficient amount of water supply should be provided to Horana export processing Zone, but the present supply is limited to 2,500 cubic metres due to technical reasons.
A water supply scheme to ensure 8,000 Cubic metres a day was planned by the BOI and the proposal made by Development Strategies and International Trade Minister Malik Samarawickrama, to transfer a land of 2 acres and 5.1 perches which is located in Maguruwela area in Horana to BOI, was approved by the Cabinet of Minister.
Establishment of a research and training complex in the Faculty of Agriculture of the University of Jaffna
Cabinet has granted approval to enter into agreements with JICA on the above purpose on April 6, 2016. The proposal made by Higher Education and Highways Minister Lakshman Kiriella, to authorize the Secretary to the Ministry to enter into an agreement with the selected contractor for implementing this project, was approved by the cabinet of Ministers.
Supply of 2,900,000 vials of anti-rabies (TC) vaccine (Human Use) 1 ml/0.5ml for prevention of rabies disease
The Proposal made by Health, Nutrition and Indigenous Medicine Minister Rajitha Senaratne, to award the above tender to the successful bidder, i.e. M/s Smithkline Beechan (pvt) Ltd, as recommended by procurement Appeal Board, was approved by the cabinet of Ministers.
Procurement supervision performance as at April 2017
According to above report, 219 Procurements were commenced in April and 151 out of them are related to the five Ministries of Higher Education and Highways, City Planning and Water Supply, Health, Nutrition and Indigenous Medicine, Defence, and Transport and Civil Aviation.
Above information presented by Finance and Mass Media Minister Mangala Samaraweera, was noted by the cabinet of Ministers along with its proposals to enhance efficiency of the procurement procedure.
Lease out a land of 3 roods and 1 perch at Sir James Peiris Mawatha, Colombo 2 to Superlative Properties Ltd for a mixed development project
The Proposal made by Megapolis and Western Development Minister Patali Champika Ranawaka, to lease out the above land for 99 years at a value of Rs. 1,815 million, to the aforesaid successful bidder, was approved by the Cabinet of ministers.
Construction of new Habarana grid substation and augmentation of Veyangoda grid substation
The Proposal made by Power and Renewable Energy Minister Ranjith Siyambalapitiya, to award the above contract to Siemens Company of India as recommended by the Standing Cabinet Appointed Procurement Committee, was approved by the Cabinet of ministers.
Amending the Assistance Agreement for Strengthened Partnership for Democratic Reform and Social Integration signed with USA
The Proposal made by Prime Minister Ranil Wickremesinghe, as National Policies and Economic Affairs Minister, to sign the above agreement after amending it to receive a total contribution of USD 26.30 million by increasing the original value of USD 22.70 million from further USD 3.6 million, was approved by the Cabinet ministers.
Regulation of the method of disposing state lands owned by public institutions
The Proposal made by President Maithripala Sirisena, to appoint a committee chaired by the Secretary to the President and comprised of Secretaries to relevant ministries and other officials, on the above matter, was approved by the Cabinet of ministers
Providing relief for lost passports of the persons distressed due to floods related disaster situation occurred in May
A surcharged of Rs. 10,000.00 should be paid to obtain a new passport for lost or destroyed passports which have not been expired.
The proposal made by Internal Affairs, Wayamba Development and Cultural Affairs Minister S.B. Navinna, to exempt victims of the flood situation from the above surcharge and to charge a concessionary fee of Rs.100.00 from persons who have obtained passports after January, on the recommendation of the Divisional Secretary and to provide these reliefs till August 30, was approved by the Cabinet of ministers.
Signing of the MoU between Sri Lanka and India for implementing Most Ven. Maduluwawe Sobhitha Nahimigama Project
Government of India has agreed to provide a grant of Rs. 296.50 million for the above project that aims at developing Elapathagama in Anuradhapura Districts, including building houses for 153 families. The proposal made by Housing and Construction Minister Sajith Premadasa, to sign the above MoU for implementing this project, was approved by the Cabinet of ministers.
Liability manage net to improve public debt management
Sri Lanka’s present debt is Rs. 9,387 billion, and it is about 76% of the income in 2016. Accordingly, the proposal made by Prime Minister Ranil Wickremesinghe, as National Policies and Economic Affairs Minister, to instruct Legal Draftsmen to draft a new act for using liability management for debt management and to take other necessary steps in this regard, was approved by the Cabinet of ministers.
Revoke of Simplified Value Added Tax (SVAT)
According, to budget proposals, Value-Added Tax (Amendment) bill was prepared by amending the effective date of the revocation as January 1, 2018. The proposal made by Finance and Mass Media Minister Mangala Samaraweera, to publish the above bill in gazette and present in parliament for approval, was approved by the Cabinet of ministers.
Waste disposal in Colombo Municipal Council and suburb areas
The proposal made by Hon. Prime Minister Ranil Wickremesinghe, for disposal of waste to recognized lands under environmental laws, take judicial action to prevent public protests on waste disposal, waste to be disposed only after grading, provide necessary tools for local government authorities and increase awareness of the general public for resolving the issues arisen in above areas, was approved by the Cabinet of ministers.
