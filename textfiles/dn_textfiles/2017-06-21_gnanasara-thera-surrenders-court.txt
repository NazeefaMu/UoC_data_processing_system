## Title: Gnanasara Thera surrenders to court
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Local
## Date: Wednesday, June 21, 2017 - 10:30

Gnanasara Thera surrenders to court

 
The Colombo Fort Magistrate recalled two warrants issued against Bodu Bala Sena (BBS) General Secretary Ven. Galaboda Atte Gnanasara Thera over two cases, after he surrendered to Court.
On June 15, Magistrate Lanka Jayaratne issued warrants for the arrest of Gnanasara Thera for not being present in Court in connection with two cases filed against him.
Gnanasara Thera surrendered to Court alone with President's Counsel Tirantha Walaliyadda through a motion application.
Walaliyadda appearing for Gnanasara Thera, moved Court that the warrant issued against his client be recalled.
He submitted to Court that his client unable to come to court because he was under death threats. 
"My client did not abscond Courts. He was under death threats," defence counsel added.
The Magistrate decided to recall the warrants against Gnanasara Thera after satisfying with suspect's previous court attendance record.
In one Court case, BBS general secretary Ven. Galaboda Atte Gnanasara Thera and seven others were named as the suspects in connection with disrupting a media conference organised by the Jathika Bala Sena (JBS) at the Nippon Hotel on April 9, 2014.
In the other case, the prosecutors alleged that the BBS General Secretary allegedly made a defamatory statement to Islam and the Holy Quranon April 12, 2014 near the Slave Island police station.
Further magisterial inquiry was fixed on August 10.
Ven. Galaboda Atte Gnanasara Thera, Ven.Vitarandeniye Nanda Thera,  ven. Welimada Chandra Ratne Thera, ven. Wellampitiye Sumana Damma Thera, Ven. Ariyawansa Sambudda Thera, Ven. Chardrananda Thera, P. Wewala and Pathiranage Gunawardena were named as the suspects in connection with disrupting the Jathika Bala Sena (JBS) media conference on April 9, 2014.
In their complaints to the police, more than eight individuals alleged that the Bodu Bala Sena General Secretary had made a defamatory statement to the media regarding Islam and the Holy Quran.
They requested police to take necessary legal action against the monk.
