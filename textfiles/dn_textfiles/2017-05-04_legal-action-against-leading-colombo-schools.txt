## Title: Legal action against leading Colombo schools
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Local
## Date: Thursday, May 4, 2017 - 01:00

Legal action against leading Colombo schools

Mosquito breeding sites in WP schools:
Health authorities have taken legal action against 106 schools in the Western Province for breeding dengue mosquitoes in school premises endangering the lives of schoolchildren, a Health Ministry spokesperson said.
According to the spokesperson, health authorities had inspected 10,089 schools in the Western Province (Colombo, Kalutara and Gampaha districts) on April 28 and 29, and detected 298 schools with mosquito larvae. Red notices had been issued for 630 schools. Schools located in 12 high risk districts face the dengue threat. The school authorities had been given one month’s time to clean up all mosquito breeding sites in their school premises and an inspection will be carried out after one month.
Legal action will be taken against school authorities who fail to clean up all mosquito breeding sites within a period of one month from the end of April, the spokesperson said. Meanwhile, the Colombo Municipal Council (CMC) sources said legal action had been taken against Principals of 17 leading schools in Colombo for breeding dengue mosquitoes inside school premises. Royal College and St. Peter’s College are among them.
