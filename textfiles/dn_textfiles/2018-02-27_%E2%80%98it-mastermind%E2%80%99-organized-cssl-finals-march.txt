## Title: ‘IT Mastermind’ organized by CSSL finals in March
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Finance
## Date: Tuesday, February 27, 2018 - 01:00

‘IT Mastermind’ organized by CSSL finals in March

xIT Mastermind’, organized by the Computer Society of Sri Lanka (CSSL) and fully sponsored by the ESOFT Metro Campus as the strategic, partner has reached the final stage in its journey, dedicated in finding the IT minds among the youth of Sri Lanka.
Out of 175 national and international schools across the country competing for the title, St. Aloysius College, Galle, Thurstan College, Colombo and Maliyadewa College, Kurunegala managed to emerge victorious after having to face several strenuous rounds, into the final arena of combat.
The grand finale will be telecast on ITN, on March 4 at 6.00 pm.
Three schools and nine great minds will be competing to become the ‘IT Mastermind 2017’
The Computer Society of Sri Lanka (CSSL) is the apex professional body representing the information and communications professional field in Sri Lanka. CSSL has been a key player in the cause of uplifting the ICT field of Sri Lanka by executing several programmes. ‘IT Mastermind’ is one core programme in the CSSL agenda which contribute in developing the interest and knowledge toward ICT among the student community of the nation.
ESOFT Metro Campus as the main sponsor of the IT Mastermind Programme is the largest ICT education institution and the mother company with an extension of 40 branches spreading nationwide and with its influence on shaping lives and creating futures spreading on an international scale.
ESOFT with a vision of treating the advancement of the ICT knowledge of the student community as a national responsibility has extended its support and assistance in granting full sponsorship to the success of the IT Mastermind programme for the 6th consecutive time.
All schools competing with a team consisting of three students each underwent a rigorous process having to succeed through several competitive rounds in order to secure its positioning in the finals. Entering into the grand finale of IT Mastermind 2018, St. Aloysius College, Galle, Thurstan College, Colombo and Maliyadewa College, Kurunegal will be facing each other in the arena where their mastery in the scope of ICT will be put to test.
The winners of IT Mastermind 2017 will be awarded with laptops worth, scholarships of Rs.100,000 each and cash prizes of Rs. 500,000.
The school that secures its position as the runners up, will be awarded with a cash prize of Rs. 200,000 and scholarships worth Rs.100,000 for each student, while the second runners up will be awarded with a cash prize of Rs. 100,000, scholarships worth Rs.100,000 for each student as take home prizes.
