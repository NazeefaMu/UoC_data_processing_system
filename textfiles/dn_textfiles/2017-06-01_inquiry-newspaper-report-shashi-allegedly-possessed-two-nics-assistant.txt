## Title: Inquiry into newspaper report that Shashi allegedly possessed two NICs: Assistant Commissioner
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Law & Order
## Date: Thursday, June 1, 2017 - 01:00

Inquiry into newspaper report that Shashi allegedly possessed two NICs: Assistant Commissioner

Giving evidence in connection with two cases filed against Shashi Weerawansa, the wife of National Freedom Front (NFF) Leader Wimal Weerawansa,an Assistant Commissioner attached to the Department of Registration of Persons informed Court that his department had initiated an inquiry into a newspaper report that Mrs. Weerawansa was possessing two National Identity Cards(NICs) by two different numbers.
The CID had filed two charge sheets against Shashi Weerawansa that she had fraudulently obtained two passports including a diplomatic passport by submitting forged documents to the Department of Immigration and Emigration.
Assistant Commissioner Indika Kumara Herath whose evidence was led by Deputy Solicitor General Dileepa Peiris informed Chief Magistrate Lal Ranasinghe Bandara that an article published on Ravaya newspaper had revealed the accused had possessed two identity cards by two different numbers indicating that she born in 1967 and 1971.
Meanwhile, the Chief Magistrate informed that the general passport and the National Identity Card (NIC) belonging to Shashi Weerawansa were not gone missing but found within the room of court’s production items. The Chief Magistrate observed that the CID had made a statement to court relying on a message of Court Registrar that the general passport and National Identity Card belonging to the accused were not found in the locker, without being consulting the officer in charge of the production items.
The case was fixed for further trial on June 22.
President’s Counsel Anil de Silva with Jayantha Weerasinghe PC appeared on behalf of the accused.
On a previous occasion, the Criminal Investigations Department (CID) filed a charge sheet against Shashi Weerawansa under two counts.
Shashi Weerawansa was arrested on February, 2015 over the charge of obtaining diplomatic and general passports illegally. She had allegedly submitted birth certificate with forged names and dates of birth to obtain those passports. She is currently out on bail.
Deputy Solicitor General Dileepa Peiris, Chief Inspector Jayantha and Police Constable Ganegedara of CID prosecuted. President’s Counsel Anil de Silva with Jayantha Weerasinghe PC appeared for the accused.
