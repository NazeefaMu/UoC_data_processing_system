## Title: Foreign Ministry marks National Integration and Reconciliation week
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Local
## Date: Wednesday, January 11, 2017 - 01:00

Foreign Ministry marks National Integration and Reconciliation week

The Foreign Affairs Ministry held a special programme on Monday to mark National Integration and Reconciliation week.
The proposal by President Maithripala Sirisena, in his capacity as the Minister of National Integration and Reconciliation, to declare the week from January 8 to 14 every year, commencing from 2017, as the national integration and reconciliation week, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers on December 21, 2016. Under the proposal a number of events are to be held island wide to promote unity, peace, understanding and mutual trust among all communities in Sri Lanka.
The National flag was hoisted and the national anthem was sung in Sinhala and Tamil, followed by the reading of the Pledge for National Integration and Reconciliation.
The importance of National Integration and reconciliation and the need to respect the country’s rich diversity of ethnicity, religion and language was highlighted at the event.
Sri Lanka Missions abroad too commenced the week, by holding events to mark the National Integration and Reconciliation week.
