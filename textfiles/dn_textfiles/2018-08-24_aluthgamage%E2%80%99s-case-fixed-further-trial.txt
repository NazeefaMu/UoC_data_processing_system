## Title: Aluthgamage’s case fixed for further trial
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Law & Order
## Date: Friday, August 24, 2018 - 01:00

Aluthgamage’s case fixed for further trial

The case filed against former Sports Minister and Kandy District MP Mahindananda Aluthgamage was yesterday fixed for further trial by the Colombo High Court.
This was for the alleged criminal breach of trust of Rs. 3.9 million funds belonging to a trade union named Sri Lanka Nidahas Kamkaru Congress.
High Court Judge Sampath Wijeratne fixed the matter for further trial on October 31 following a defence request that they were yet to receive previous case proceedings.
The witnesses of the case were advised to appear before court on next the trial date.
Meanwhile, the High Court permitted MP Aluthgamage to travel to China between September 9 and 17. The Attorney General alleged that former Sports Minister Aluthgamage had committed a criminal breach of trust with regard to the misappropriation of Rs. 1,000,000, Rs. 1,900,000 and another Rs. 1,000,000 belonging to the Sri Lanka Nidahas Kamkaru Congress between October 2012 and April 2013.
The Attorney General charged that the accused had committed punishable offences under Sections 388 and 389 of the Penal Code, listed 13 persons as witnesses in the case and named eight documents as production items.
Counsel Kanchana Ratwatte with Counsel Hafeel Farisz and Dinesh Liyanage appeared for the accused. Deputy Solicitor General Thusith Mudalige appeared for the Attorney General.
