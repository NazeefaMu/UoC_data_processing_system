## Title: Western and Southern win men’s and women’s titles
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Sports
## Date: Monday, September 12, 2016 - 01:00

Western and Southern win men’s and women’s titles

Western Province bagged the men’s Cup championship beating Central Province by 17 points (2 tries, 2 conversions, 1 penalty) to 12 (2 tries, 1 conversion) while Southern Province clinched the women’s title defeating Sabaragamuwa Province by 17 (2 tries, 2 conversion, 1 penalty) to 12 (2 tries, 1 conversion) in the finals of the National Sports Festival Rugby Sevens championship concluded at the Race Course International Rugby Stadium yesterday.
Romesh Rukmal Fernando of Western Province was adjudged the men’s Best Player and P. Dulani of Southern Province was picked as the women’s Best Player. Western Province secured third place in the women’s championship.
The men’s Plate championship was won by Southern Province who beat Sabaragamuwa Province in a tough battle 10-7. Northern Western Province cruised to an easy victory over North Central Province to bag the men’s Bowl championship 31-10, while the Northern Province walked away with the Shield championship taming Uva Province 5-0.
The two-day tournament marked a milestone in Sri Lankan rugby history with rugby’s inaugural inclusion at the National Sports Festival. All nine provinces were represented in the men’s category while three teams from Southern, Sabaragamuwa and Western provinces took part in the women’s championship. It was a surprise that Rugby crazy Central Province did not field a women’s team for the battle.
