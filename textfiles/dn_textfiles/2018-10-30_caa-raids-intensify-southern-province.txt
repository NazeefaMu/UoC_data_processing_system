## Title: CAA raids intensify in Southern Province
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Local
## Date: Tuesday, October 30, 2018 - 01:00

CAA raids intensify in Southern Province

Investigation officials attached to the Consumer Affairs Authority of the Southern Province have enhanced their raids against errant traders in the region.
According to Southern Provincial Assistant Director of the Consumer Affairs Authority S. Nandasiri, investigation officials of the CAA in the Southern Region have recorded revenue of over Rs. 7million by way of court fines from fraudulent traders during the last nine months. The raids were conducted at Divisional Secretariat Divisions level in the districts of Galle, Matara and Hambantota.
The number of raids carried out in the Southern region during this period is 1,864. Of these, the highest number of 677 raids was made in Galle.
During the raids and inspections, instances of sale and display of food items unsuitable for human consumption, sale and display of expired food, fraudulently changing set prices on labels and selling them at excessive rates, selling electrical appliances without issuing warranty cards, sale of cement above set prices, non-display of price lists, hoarding essential consumer items and violating orders, rules and regulations imposed by the Consumer Affairs Authority were offences detected by the investigation officials. Errant vendors were prosecuted in the Magistrate’s Courts in Galle, Matara and Hambantota, officials said.
The authority receives complaints on fraud, malpractice and corruption committed by unscrupulous traders daily. Under these circumstances, the Consumer Affairs Authority will intensify raids to bring fraudulent traders to book, CAA officials added.
The raids were conducted on the orders of Industries and Commerce Minister and under the supervision of District Secretaries of Galle, Matara and Hambantota, Southern Regional Assistant Director of the CAA added.
