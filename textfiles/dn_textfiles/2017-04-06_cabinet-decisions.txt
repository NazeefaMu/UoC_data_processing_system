## Title: Cabinet Decisions
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Political
## Date: Thursday, April 6, 2017 - 01:00

Cabinet Decisions

Government local office costs of UN Development Programme
UNDP works closely with Sri Lanka since 1967. Sri Lanka contributes USD 150,000 as the combined voluntary subscription and USD 39500 as expenses for UNDP office. As Sri Lanka was classified as a middle income country in 2014 from a low middle income country, Sri Lanka should have settled a due of USD 1.182 million on 31-12-2016. The proposal made by Prime Minister Ranil Wickremesinghe, in his capacity as the Minister of National Policies and Economic Affairs, to implement the proposal made by UNDP to implement identified development programmes using that money instead of repaying the due amount, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
Development of Colombo as a cultural hub in South Asia
More than 2 million tourists have visited Sri Lanka in 2016, and 70 percent of them arrive with the purpose of recreation, while 25 percent for visiting their relatives or friends. Although there is a potential in attracting tourists by our traditional cultural aspects, only 1 percent of tourists arrive in Sri Lanka with religious and cultural aspects. Accordingly, the proposal made by Prime Minister Ranil Wickremesinghe, in his capacity as the Minister of National Policies and Economic Affairs, to appoint a committee of senior officials for taking necessary action for developing Colombo city as a performing and exhibition hub in South Asia for promoting domestic arts and cultural elements, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
Rehabilitation of 20 national highways
About 7,900 km of highways of a total length of 12,210 km have been already rehabilitated and about another 4,300 km are to be rehabilitated. It has been planned to rehabilitate 20 highways of A and B classes with a total length of 564.53 km at an estimated cost of Rs. 58 billion, within the next 03 years. Considering the importance of capacity-development of local contractors and the ability to use domestic funds through local banks, the proposal made by Minister of Higher Education and Highways Lakshman Kiriella, to call for proposals from interested local companies under the Swiss Challenge procurement method, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
Implementing the Kandy tunnel construction project
It has been proposed to construct a 4-lane roadway which comprises of tunnels of total length 4.36 km and 1.2 km connecting Tennekumbura and Suduhumpola to reduce the traffic congestion in Kandy. Estimated cost is USD 252.30 million. Economic Development Cooperation Fund of Korea has agreed to provide financial facilities of USD 199.27 for the project. The proposal made by Minister of Higher Education and Highways Lakshman Kiriella, to implement this project and authorize the Department of External Resources to negotiate regarding regulations and conditions of the loan, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
Purchasing land for development activities of Sri Jayewardenepura University
About 11,557 students study in 7 faculties of the USJ. It has been planned to purchase surrounding buildings and lands for its development activities. Accordingly, the proposal made by Minister of Higher Education and Highways Lakshman Kiriella to purchase a land of 377.5 perches, owned by a private party, using a portion of earnings of the university and government funds, for Rs. 481.31 million, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
Establishment of new vocational training centre in Panadura
About 9,000 students annually leave schools after sitting for OL and AL examinations in Kalutara District, but only 1,785 can be enrolled to training centres of Vocational Training Authority. Accordingly, the proposal made by Minister of Skills Development and Vocational Training Mahinda Samarasinghe to establish a new vocational training centre in Panadura area at an estimated cost of Rs. 370 million, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
Programme utilizing uncultivated paddy fields for promotion of freshwater aquaculture activities
Aquaculture currently contributes 13 percent of the total fresh water fish production of Sri Lanka. It has been identified that many uncultivated paddy fields in wet and dry zones are suitable for aquaculture, and it could increase the production by 10,000 T and earn an additional income of Rs. 2.5 billion. Accordingly, the proposal made by Minister of Fisheries and Aquatic Resources Development Mahinda Amaraweera, to use suitable uncultivated paddy fields in this regard after a proper feasibility study, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
Amendment to assist to and protect victims of crime and witnesses act no. 04 of 2015
The proposal made by Minister of Justice Wijayadasa Rajapaksa, to publish in gazette and present in parliament for approval the bill drafted to amend the above act, making provisions to enable victims and witnesses to give evidence from outside the country through audio-visual media, without being present at court, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
Importation of 20,000 dairy heifers
Under the above project, the first stock of heifers will be received by Sri Lanka on May 17, 2017. They will be provided for farmers to a concessionary price and required funds will be provided by Central Bank of Sri Lanka under a relief loan scheme. Beneficiaries for the first stage of the programme have been selected, and the project should provide employment opportunities for at least two samurdhi beneficiaries in a farm with minimum of 10 heifers. Above proposal made by Minister of Rural Economic Affairs P. Harrison, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
Revolving recoveries of the relief loan scheme to people displaced by war
Above loan scheme was implemented according to a cabinet decision made on August 11, 2010, through Bank of Ceylon, and on December 31, 2016, loans of Rs. 770.1 million were granted to 5,222 beneficiaries and Rs. 497 million were recovered. As the BOC expects to revolve the recoveries to resettled persons, the proposal made by Minister of Prison Reforms, Rehabilitation, Resettlement, and Hindu Religious Affairs D.M. Swaminathan, to do so, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
Setting up bilingual signage in public/semi-government institutions and public places
Under the Official Language Policy any, person should have the ability of reach where he needs through bilingual signs and boards, and in all public institutions, all name boards should be demonstrated bilingually and services in reception counters should be provided in both languages. Accordingly, the proposal made by Minister of National Coexistence, Dialogue, and Official Languages Mano Ganeshan, to guide public institutions to rectify any deficiencies regarding implementation of official languages policy, and to provide any required additional funds through the Ministry of National Coexistence, Dialogue, and Official Languages, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
Memorandums of Understanding to be signed between Ministry of Primary Industries and Bank of Agriculture and Agricultural Cooperatives (Thailand) and Faculty of agriculture Kasetsart University (Thailand)
The proposal made by Minister of Primary Industries Daya Gamage, to sign the above two MoU, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
Central Expressway project – Section 1 from Kadawatha to Mirigama (36.59 km) – award of contract for providing consultancy services for design review and construction supervision
The proposal made by Minister of Higher Education and Highways Lakshman Kiriella, to award the above contract as recommended by the Cabinet Appointed Consultant Procurement Committee at a cost of Rs. 2548.91 million to the joint venture of Resources Development Consultants (Pvt) Ltd, Green Tech Consultant (Pvt) Ltd, and Consulting Engineers and Architects Associates (Pvt) Ltd, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
Revision of total cost estimate of Ruhunupura Water supply project
The proposal made by Minister of City Planning and Water Supply Rauff Hakeem, to revise the original estimate of Rs. 8653.54 million of the above project as Rs. 10,029.64 million due to factors beyond control of contractor, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
Construction of a special paediatric unit in Karapitiya Teaching Hospital
As the third largest hospital in Sri Lanka, Karapitiya Teaching Hospital, has 60 wards with 1,900 beds, but only 250 beds are allocated for child patients. It has been decided to construct a building complex to establish a complete Paediatric Unit, and accordingly, the proposal made by Minister of Health Nutrition and Indigenous Medicine Dr. Rajitha Senarathne, to award the above contract to Central Engineering Consultancy Bureau and Engineering Services Private Limited subject to recommendations of Cabinet Appointed Procurement Committee, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
Procurement for supply, installation, and commissioning of 137 Nos. anaesthetic machines with ventilators
The proposal made by Minister of Health Nutrition and Indigenous Medicine Dr. Rajitha Senarathne, to award the above contract as recommended by the Standing Cabinet Appointed Procurement Committee, at a cost of USD 1.3 million to M/s Meditechnology Holdings (Pvt) Ltd, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
Construction of corporate office building of distribution division 4
The proposal made by Minister of Power and Renewable Energy Ranjith Siyambalapitiya, to award the above contract as recommended by Procurement Appeal Board at a cost of Rs. 466.66 million without tax to the lowest responsive bidder M/s. Sripalie Contractors (Pvt) limited, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
Revision of scope of the Mannar Grid substation construction
Above contract was awarded in 2014 and its scope has been revised because the capacity of substation should be increased from 132 kV to 220 kV, and new financial proposals have been called in this regard. According to agreements made by the Standing Cabinet Appointed Procurement Committee, the proposal made by Minister of Power and Renewable Energy Ranjith Siyambalapitiya, to obtain the services of the revised scope from the current contractor, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
Implementation of 'Viru Sumithuru' housing project for families of war heroes
Above project expects to build 1,950 new houses and to complete partially-built 1,700 houses. Beneficiaries will be identified by Ranaviru Seva Authority. Rs. 1.2 million will be provided for a complete house and Rs. 500,000 will be provided for partially-built houses and the three forces and police will contribute required labour. Accordingly, the joint proposal made by President Maithripala Sirisena Minister of Housing and Construction Sajith Premadasa, to implement this project during 2017-2020, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
Smallholder agribusiness partnerships programme to assist rural agri-producers under the assistance of low interest credit facility of international fund for agriculture development
Above programme will benefit 57,500 low-income families within the next 6 years, and it will be operated by President's Office with an investment of USD 105 million. Accordingly, the proposal made by President Maithripala Sirisena, to implement the project and to authorize the Department of External Resources to hold relevant loan discussions, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.Accordingly, the proposal made by Prime Minister Ranil Wickremesinghe, in his capacity as the Minister of National Policies and Economic Affairs, to select students based on z-score of 2014 and 2015 AL results and to implement its first step through National School of Business Management, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
Further assistance for the persons affected due to landslide in Kegalle District
The National Building Research Institute has recommended evacuating and resettling people living in 1,682 houses due to high rainfall in May, 2016. The amount of houses has been increased to 1,941 by later researches and the proposal made by Minister of Disaster Management Anura Priyadarshana Yapa, to allocate additional funds from treasury for resettlement of 259 families, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
Income tax incentives for investors
The proposal made by Minister of Finance Ravi Karunanayake, to instruct the Legal Draftsman to draft the proposed Income Tax Act by including schedule ii. 'Investment Incentive Scheme' and schedule iv. 'Incentive scheme for small and medium investors, who invest for the period from 2017 to 2019, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
