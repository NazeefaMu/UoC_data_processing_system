## Title: New SLFP office-bearers appointed
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Local
## Date: Monday, June 4, 2018 - 01:05

New SLFP office-bearers appointed

The SLFP’s key decision making bodies – the Executive Council and All Island Committee yesterday appointed a new set of office-bearers to handle future party activities and decided to carry out party reorganisation.
The joint meeting of the Sri Lanka Freedom Party’s Executive Council and All Island Committee was held under the patronage of party leader and President Maithripala Sirisena at the Ape Gama premises in Battaramulla yesterday morning.
President Maithripala Sirisena was re-elected party President while Professor Rohana Lakshman Piyadasa was elected as the party’s General Secretary replacing Minister Duminda Dissanayake.
Minister Dissanayake, meanwhile was appointed as the SLFP National Organiser and former Minister S.B. Dissanayake was elected as Party Treasurer.
A press release by the President’s Media Unit said two former Presidents and a Prime Minister were appointed to the Party Advisory Committee.
Accordingly, former President Chandrika Bandaranaike Kumaratunga, former President Mahinda Rajapaksa and former Prime Minister D.M. Jayaratne will form the Party Advisory Committee.
The SLFP will have four Senior Vice Presidents including Minister Nimal Siripala De Silva, former Ministers W.D.J. Seneviratne, Anura Priyadarshana Yapa and Susil Premajayantha.
A.H.M. Fowzie, Sarath Amunugama, Mahinda Amaraweera, Dayasiri Jayasekara, Reginold Cooray, Piyasena Gamage, Vijith Vijayamuni de Soyza, Mahinda Samarasinghe, Dilan Perera, Southern Province Chief Minister Shan Vijayalal de Silva, MP Angajan Ramanadan were elected as SLFP Vice Presidents.
Several others were elected as new Deputy Secretaries of the party. They are, Minister Ranjith Siyambalapitiya, Central Province Chief Minister Sarath Ekanayake, North-Western Province Chief Minister Dharmasiri Dasanayake, Western Province Chief Minister Isura Devapriya, Uva Province Chief Minister Chamara Sampath Dasanayake, Deputy Minister Lasantha Alagiyawanna, former Deputy Minister Dr. Sudarshanee Fernandopulle and Sumedha G. Jayasena.
All Parliamentarians representing the SLFP at present were elected to the party Central Committee. The Party President will make other appointments to the CC shortly. The party organisers, professionals, artists and various other important organisations and their affiliated organisation representing the fivefold forces, the Sangha, native physicians, teachers, farmers and workers to be established according to the party constitution are due to be appointed in the near future.
Minister Mahinda Amaraweera responding to the Daily News said the main focus of the joint meeting was on party reorganisation that will ensure the SLFP’s victory at the Provincial Council Election which would be held in the end of this year. Minister Amaraweera also said some of the new appointments were done on a temporary basis.
He said that the SLFP members had not discussed anything about leaving the coalition government and added the focus was on restructuring the party to meet future challenges.
A member of the SLFP Group of 16 who recently decided relinquish their posts and sit in the opposition, SLFP Senior Vice President, John Seneviratne MP told Daily News that President Maithripala Sirisena and the new General Secretary spoke at the joint meeting while former General Secretary delivered the welcome speech.
“It was pointed out that SLFP has contributed much to the betterment of the general public of this country. We should not allow it to get destroyed, it was also pointed out. It was also said that the party needs to be reorganised and strengthen before taking any crucial decision,” MP Seneviratne speaking on the discussion content said.
Asked if the SLFP Group 16 made any special observations at the joint meeting, Seneviratne said that they did not, as the group of 16 is still a part of the Sri Lanka Freedom Party. “We walked out of the government because we disagreed with some policies taken up by it. But we are still a part of the SLFP,” he said.
Seneviratne said that during discussions several members had pointed out that the SLFP lost at the last LG elections as the economic measures of the government had been rejected by the people.
Also he said several members had expressed that the the SLFP’s political alignment with the UNP too had hurt the party at the polls.

New appointments on a temporary basis - President
Amali Mallawaarachchi
President Maithripala Sirisena yesterday said that the Sri Lanka Freedom Party (SLFP) had appointed office-bearers on a temporary basis to carry out party reorganisation activities.
Speaking to journalists after the joint meeting of the SLFP Executive Council and All Island Committee at the Ape Gama premises in Battaramulla, the President said the SLFP expects to complete party reorganisation within 45 days.
He also said the new appointments had been approved unanimously by the SLFP members of the two party decision making bodies.
****
SLFP will be re-organised - General Secretary Prof. Piyadasa
The newly appointed SLFP General Secretary Professor Rohana Lakshman Piyadasa yesterday said the party will be strengthened and re-organised in line with the people’s expectations in a short period.
He said in line with President Maithripala Sirisena’s vision, the SLFP will focus on ushering a better economy which is production and people oriented and based on indigenous thinking.
He said the SLFP will be strengthened as a powerful people’s movement to realise the above goals.
“I have an enormous responsibility as the new SLFP General Secretary. We have to reorganise, rebuild and strengthen this party. Most importantly, we must champion our party policies,” he said.
Piyadasa said the SLFP expects to complete party reorganisation within 45 days and achieve success at the upcoming elections.
