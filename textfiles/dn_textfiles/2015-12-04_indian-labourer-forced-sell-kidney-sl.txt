## Title: Indian labourer forced to sell kidney in SL
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Local
## Date: Friday, December 4, 2015 - 01:00

Indian labourer forced to sell kidney in SL

Police in Akola in Maharashtra, India have busted an international kidney racket by arresting a moneylender who had forced a daily wage labourer to travel to Sri Lanka to sell off a kidney to repay a loan of Indian Rs. 20,000, Daily News and Analysis (DNA) reported yesterday.
According to a report, the kidney racket may be linked to farmer suicides in Vidarbha. A spate of farmer suicides in the region has alarmed India, but part of the reason is the moneylenders' exploitation poor farmers and labourers.
