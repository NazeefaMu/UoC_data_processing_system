## Title: POW open T20 tourney with a good win over Lumbini
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Sports
## Date: Monday, April 4, 2016 - 01:00

POW open T20 tourney with a good win over Lumbini

 
Prince of Wales College, Moratuwa recorded a 28-run win over Lumbini College. Colombo in one of the pre-quarter final matches concluded on Thursday at Colts Ground booking a quarter-final spot in U19 T20 tournament.
Prince of Wales after electing to bat first failed to make a challenging total before they were bowled out for an unimpressive total of 114 in just 18 overs, but their bowlers did well to restrict Lumbini to a total of 86 for nine in their 20 overs after a tight bowling attack to win the match by 28 runs in the end.
Prince of Wales : 114 in 18 overs (Kasun Ekanayake 35, Sanoj Dharshika 26, Mohommed Akram 2/18, Thanuka Dabare 2/17)
Lumbini: 86/9 in 20 overs (Chanaka Devinda 27*, Vimukthi Kulathunga 14, Savindu Peiris 3/21)
St. Aloysius' in quarters
St.Aloysius' College, Galle who created a few upsets in this year's U19 tournament pulled off another astonishing win when they defeated powerhouse Isipathana by two wickets in their pre-quarter final match ended at Mahinda ground on Thursday.
Isipathana : 104 in 19.5 overs (Vishad Randika 25, Pramodh Madhuwantha 17, Ravindu Sanjana 4/10, Sajith Sankalpa 4/17)
St. Aloysius': 106/8 in 19.4 overs (Gihan Niroshan 37*, Navindu Nirmal 17, Lahiru Dilshan 3/19)
Sachitha shines in Thomian win
All-rounder Sachitha Jayatilleke scored 47 useful runs and claimed three dismissals with the ball as S.Thomas', Mt.Lavinia surged to quarter finals of Singer U19 T20 tournament beating Gurukula by 15 runs in their pre-quarter final fixture concluded on Thursday in Mt.Lavinia.
S. Thomas': 134 in 20 overs (Sachitha Jayatilleke 47, Yohan Mendis 32, Jayamal Wanniarachchi 4/36, Tharindu Tharaka 2/25, Malindu Viduranga 2/21)
Gurukula : 119 in 18.2 overs (Lasindu Arosha 61, S. Fernando 2/32, Sachitha Jayatilleke 3/15, Sithara Hapuhinna 2/13)
Richmond oust Royal
Royal College, Colombo were brought down to earth by Richmond's efficient set of bowlers playing a big part in their side toppling high-riding Royal by 8 wickets in their pre-quarter final fixture of Schools T20 tournament 2016 concluded at Royal ground on Friday.
Royal : 89 in 19 overs (Ronuka Jayawardana 24, Kavindu Madarasingha 23, Awindu Theekshana 4/16, Ravishka Wijesiri 3/14)
Richmond : 93/2 in 14.3 overs (Charith Asalanka 33, Wanidu Hasaranga 32*)
St.Sebastian's advance to quarter-finals
Thurstan struggled for runs and were shot out for a total that was modest in the conditions, 96 all out in just under 17 overs, easily chased down by St.Sebastian's in a little less than 14 overs to win their pre-quarter final fixture concluded at Thurstan ground on Saturday sealing their place in quarter-finals of Singer U19 Schools T20 Tournament.
Thurstan: 96 in 16.3 overs (Isitha Wijesundara 27, Asher Warnakulasuriya 3/10, Tashik Perera 2/21, Nimesh Bandara 2/19)
St.Sebastian's : 97/4 in 13.3 overs (Nishitha Abhilash 37, Mishen Silva 20*, Nipun Lakshan 3/21)
St.Aloysius' stun S.Thomas'
St.Aloysius College, Galle after they knocked batsmen-packed Isipathana out of the tournament in pre-quarter finals booked their place in semi-finals beating another power-house, S.Thomas' Mt.Lavinia in their quarter-final fixture of Singer Under 19 Schools T20 Tournament, concluded on Saturday at Thurstan ground.
St.Aloysius' :105 in 20 overs (Navindu Nirmal 21, Sajith Sankalpa 21, Harin Buddila 22, Rashmika Opatha 3/12, Sachitha Jayathilaka 3/17)
S.Thomas' : 92 in 19.1 overs (Yohan Mendis 34, Hareen Buddila 3/11, Ravindu Sanjana 2/10, Gihan Niroshan 2/17)
