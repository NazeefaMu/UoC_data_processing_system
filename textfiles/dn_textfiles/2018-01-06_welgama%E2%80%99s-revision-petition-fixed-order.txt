## Title: Welgama’s revision petition fixed for order
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Law & Order
## Date: Saturday, January 6, 2018 - 01:00

Welgama’s revision petition fixed for order

A revision petition filed by former Transport Minister Kumara Welgama challenging the Magistrate’s Court’s decision to accept an amended charge sheet over a corruption case was fixed for order by the Colombo High Court.
Colombo High Court Judge Vikum Kaluarachchi fixed the matter for order on January 30.
Welgama is accused of causing the government an unlawful loss by appointing an individual as a Deputy Chairman of Sri Lanka Transport Board (SLTB), contrary to the Transport Act.
The Director General of the Commission to Investigate Allegations of Bribery or Corruption had filed a case in Colombo Chief Magistrate’s Court against former Minister Welgama under Section 70 of the Bribery Act on four charges.
The Bribery Commission alleged that the former Minister had unlawfully appointed L.A. Wimalaratne as the Deputy Chairman of the SLTB on June 23, 2010, as Transport Minister.
The Bribery Commission further alleged that Welgama had paved the way for confer a payment of Rs.3.2 million to the Deputy Chairman as his salary and other benefits, causing unlawful loss to the Government.
