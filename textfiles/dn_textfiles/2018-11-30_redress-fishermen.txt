## Title: Redress for fishermen
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Local
## Date: Friday, November 30, 2018 - 01:00

Redress for fishermen

Prime Minister Mahinda Rajapaksa directed relevant officials to find urgent solutions to problems encountered by the Fisheries sector.
He issued this directive at a meeting with representatives of Fisheries unions on Tuesday. Representatives of the Northern Eastern Province Fisheries unions, Multi-Day Boat Fisheries unions and small-scale fishermen’s unions attended the meeting.
Problems confronting fishermen such as high production costs, lack of equipment at fair prices, and problems relating to fisheries harbours and boats gained the attention of the Prime Minister at this meeting.
The fishermen also requested the Premier to reduce the import price of fibre and other raw materials.
The Prime Minister advised the Fisheries Minister to modernise the Fisheries Corporation cold rooms. He advised the Minister to ensure that income earned by fishermen is exempted from income tax.
The Prime Minister also directed the Minister to devise a life insurance scheme for fishermen, and provide two mother ships and lifeboats to rescue fishermen in distress.
Fisheries Minister Vijith Vijayamuni Zoysa, former Fisheries Minister Mahinda Amaraweera, Sudarshani Fernandopulle, Minister Douglas Devananda, Parliamentarians Dulip Wijesekera and Arundika Fernando, Secretary to the Prime Minister, S. Amarasekera and other officials were also present on the occasion.
