## Title: Special discussion regarding dengue vaccine held
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Local
## Date: Wednesday, August 30, 2017 - 01:00

Special discussion regarding dengue vaccine held

A special discussion was held at the Health Ministry recently in connection with the possibility of introducing a vaccination for dengue. Global Dengue Expert Prof. Prof. Scott B. Halsted was also present during the discussion, Health Ministry spokesman said.
According to the spokesman, Health Minister Dr. Rajitha Senaratne, National Medicines Regulatory Authority (NMRA) Chairman Prof. Asitha De Silva, Centre for Clinical Management of Dengue and Dengue Hemorrhagic Fever Clinical Head Dr. Lakkumara Fernando and National Dengue Control Programme National Coordinator Dr. Hasitha Tissera were present during the discussion.
The main objective of the discussion was finding out the suitability of the dengue vaccine now available in the Singapore market. It was decided to find out the suitability of the vaccine through scientific research, studies and observations before taking a decision on permitting to registration the dengue vaccine in Sri Lanka, he said.
During the discussion, it was revealed that the number of dengue patients have been decreased now. A total of 144,052 dengue patients had been reported from all parts of the country by August 25, he added.
