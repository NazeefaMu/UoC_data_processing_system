## Title: President assigns flood affected districts to ministers
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Local
## Date: Friday, June 2, 2017 - 01:00

President assigns flood affected districts to ministers

President Maithripala Sirisena at a special meeting with members of his government yesterday assigned affected districts to ministers to ensure that they are brought back to normalcy within a period of two months. The meeting which had been summoned at the Presidential Secretariat sought to assess the progress of the government machinery in dealing with the current disaster situation in the country.
It also laid special emphasis on the role played by each of the ministers, state ministers and deputy ministers in relief activities in their relevant electorates.
The President had asked ministers from districts which have not been affected by the floods to visit those which have been affected and help in coordination and management of the disaster situation.
They are to assist the politicians of the respective areas. Primary Industries Minister Daya Gamage who hails from the Digamadulla District aid he had been assigned responsibility of the Kegalle district, “Some were also assigned the worst affected electorates which need additional attention,”he added. The President asked that we all work as a team to ensure that full recovery is made, explained the Minister. “It is like being given the responsibility of a ministry,”he said.
The recent floods wreaked havoc in the Colombo, Gampaha, Kegalle, Kaluthara, Galle, Matara, Hambantota and Ratnapura districts.
