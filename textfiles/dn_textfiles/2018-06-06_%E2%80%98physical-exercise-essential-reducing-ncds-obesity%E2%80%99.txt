## Title: ‘Physical exercise essential for reducing NCDs, obesity’
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Local
## Date: Wednesday, June 6, 2018 - 01:00

‘Physical exercise essential for reducing NCDs, obesity’

Non-Communicable Diseases (NCDs) have become a global obstacles and it is a challenge here in Sri Lanka as well, Health Minister Dr. Rajitha Senaratne said.
Addressing the launch of the National Nutrition Month (June) held in Colombo, recently, he said that the theme of this year’s National Nutrition Month is The Proper Way to Reduce Belly Fat. The main reason for obesity is lack of physical exercise and unhealthy food habits.
Minister Dr. Senaratne added that two surveys conducted in 2010 and 2015 on the obesity levels among Sri Lankan men and women, revealed that numbers of individuals either overweight or obese, had risen during that five-year period.
The minister said that since President Maithripala Sirisena and he himself allocate time for physical exercise, the rest of the country should find time to exercise as well, in order to prevent NCDs and from becoming overweight.
Health Minister Dr. Rajitha Senaratne, Health Ministry Secretary Janaka Sugathadasa, Health Services Director General Dr. Anil Jasinghe and others participated in physical exercises during the event.
