## Title: Navin’s matter resolved: Harin
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Political
## Date: Friday, September 2, 2016 - 01:00

Navin’s matter resolved: Harin

 
 The issue over which Plantation Industries Minister Navin Dissanayake threatened to resign has been resolved, Telecommunication and Digital Infrastructure   Minister Harin Fernando said.
He was responding to a question by a journalist at Sirikotha yesterday. “Minister Dissanayake had spoken to President Maithripala Sirisena and Prime Minister Ranil Wickremesinghe,” he said. “The problem has been sorted. It was informed at the last Cabinet meeting that they had come to a consensus on the matter.
“It was informed that Minister Dissanayake would not resign. Minister Dissanayake was also asked not to repeat his previous statement anymore. “Thus that chapter has been closed,” he said. Minister Dissanayake threatened to resign from his ministerial portfolio over the move by the Treasury to acquire monies collected by way of CESS ,belonging to the Tea Board. Minister Fernando clarified that it is a temporary measure until December as the Government was need of funds to run the economy. “The Finance Minister explained that this is a short term plan as the budget deficit has to be controlled and the IMF deadlines have to be met.
"The Finance Ministry is also burdened with some unexpected loans obtained during the previous regime which had not been accounted,” he stated. Minister Dissanayake was to attend the press conference scheduled at 10.30 am, however he had later informed that he could not come as he was unwell. Minister Fernando attended the press conference around 11 am in place of Dissanayake.
