## Title: Vote on Budget 2016 Third Reading today
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Political
## Date: Saturday, December 19, 2015 - 01:00

Vote on Budget 2016 Third Reading today

 
The Vote on the Third Reading of Budget 2016 is scheduled to be taken up at 5.00 pm in Parliament today. "President Maithripala Sirisena will be present in Parliament today," sources said.
This is the consensual government's first full budget which was presented to Parliament on November 20 by Finance Minister Ravi Karunanayake with total government expenditure for the next year estimated at Rs 1,941 billion (1,941,450,438,000).
The Second Reading of the Appropriation Bill presented to Parliament on October 24, by Leader of the House Minister Lakshman Kiriella commenced on the following day and continued till December 2.
The vote on the Second Reading of the budget was held on December 2 and it was passed with a majority of 107 votes. Fifteen full days from December 3 to December 19 have been allocated for the Third Reading Stage debate on the budget proposals. However,due to protests and opposition made against some of the proposed budget proposals, the President and Prime Minister have made some amendments to the original proposals.
According to sources, members of the joint Opposition have decided to vote against the Third Reading of the 2016 budget.
