## Title: Buddhist and Pali University stage Sahithya Sankathana
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Local
## Date: Friday, September 15, 2017 - 01:00

Buddhist and Pali University stage Sahithya Sankathana

The Buddhist and Pali University in its 35 years has done immense service to the country and Buddhism by producing well-qualified scholars to disseminate Buddhism in the local and international communities. Similarly, the university gives prime importance to the academic growth of the students as well as the academic staff.
Sahithya Sankathana, the literary arts festival was first introduced to the university in 2016 as per a concept of the Vice Chancellor, Ven. Senior Professor Gallelle Sumanasiri Thera for the second consecutive year.
A series of events have commenced yestarday and today at the university premises, Pitipana, Homagama. The inauguration ceremony was held yesterday (14) at 9.00 am and a special discussion on “Practical Buddhism and Theravada Tradition” was held at 10.00 a.m.
The creative skills of the academic staff, non-academic staff and students was showcased at 12.30 p.m. and the refereed journals, journals and magazines of each department and students’ association and the unpublished books of academic members was also launched.
The second day of the festival also comprises some valuable events like a discussion on the “Challenges in the field of Buddhist Studies” at 9.00 a.m., a food festival at 11 a.m., prize giving and certificate awarding at 1.00 a.m., the progress assessment of the university and a book donation for Dhamma schools and libraries at 3 p.m., and the most entertaining poetic soiree at 6.00 p.m. The main objective of organizing this event is to make the students aware of different food cultures in other countries such as China, Japan, Korea, France and India.
This food exhibition is an imposing practical activity of the foreign language students of the university which could deepen their knowledge of various multicultural culinary activities.
