## Title: Political parties ready to hold May Day rallies today
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Political
## Date: Monday, May 7, 2018 - 01:00

Political parties ready to hold May Day rallies today

Many political parties will hold May Day rallies today. The Sri Lanka Freedom Party (SLFP) May Day rally will be held in Chenkalady, Batticaloa with the participation of SLFP Leader and President Maithripala Sirisena.
The SLFP May Day rally will be held under the theme, ‘Promoting Reconciliation and Inter-Communal Harmony'. The party stalwarts and the 16 SLFP members who quit the party will participate in the rally. Minister Nimal Siripala de Silva said those MPs had assured their participation and the objective of the May Day rally to be held in the Eastern province is to unite all factions of the party.
The United National Party (UNP) May Day Rally was held at the Sugathadasa Stadium yesterday morning.
The Joint Opposition (JO) May Day rally will be held at the Samanala grounds, Galle, in the Southern Province led by former President Mahinda Rajapaksa, with the participation of all its allied parties today.
Unbearable tax burden on the people will be one of the main themes of the rally and it would also protest against rising inflation, Government’s moves to sell national resources and political victimization.
The Janatha Vimukthi Peramuna (JVP) main May Day rally will be held at the BRC Grounds. The JVP held its first rally in Jaffna on May 1 despite the government's decision to hold the May Day today since the Vesak Full Moon Poya Day fell on May 1.
The JVP procession will commence from the Campbell Park at 1.00 p.m. and gather at the BRC Ground for its rally. The rally will be under the theme, ‘People’s Action for Social Justice and National Unity’.
The Jathika Hela Urumaya (JHU) is to hold its May Day Rally at the New Town Hall, Colombo today with the participation of its General Secretary Minister Patali Champika Ranawaka and Co-chairman and Deputy Minister Karunaratne Paranavithana.
The JHU May Day rally will be under the theme, 'The May Day Rally of the People Toiling Under the Sun and Rain,' would commence the procession in front of the Colombo Public Library at 9 am and gather at the New Town Hall at 10 a.m. for the rally.
Thousand workers would be granted insurance policies at the JHU rally.
