## Title: AG files fresh money laundering case against Wele Suda
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Law & Order
## Date: Friday, October 26, 2018 - 01:00

AG files fresh money laundering case against Wele Suda

The Attorney General has filed a fresh money laundering case against two accused including controversial drug dealer Gampola Vidanage Samantha Kumara alias Wele Suda for allegedly laundering black money to the tune of Rs. 31 million.
The Attorney General had filed indictments against the two accused Gampola Vidanage Samantha Kumara and Sameera Udara for laundering of money earned through drug trafficking, to the tune of Rs. 31 million between December 2011 and December 2012, under two charges under the Prevention of Money Laundering Act.
When the case came up before High Court Judge Sampath Abeykoon, accused Sameera Udara was ordered to be released on a cash bail of Rs.150,000 with two sureties of one million rupees.
Convicted drug dealer Wele Suda who was sentenced to death by the Colombo High Court over another case was produced before Court through the prison authorities.
