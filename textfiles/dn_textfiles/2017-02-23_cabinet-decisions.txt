## Title: Cabinet Decisions
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Political
## Date: Thursday, February 23, 2017 - 01:00

Cabinet Decisions

Accelerating compensation payment for land owners affected by Moragahakanda – Kalu Ganga project
Considering the facts presented regarding the issues faced by land owners whose lands are in the area covered by the reservoir, the proposal made by President Maithripala Sirisena in his capacity as the Mahaweli Development and Environment Minister, to pay the complete compensation within 2017-2018, was approved by the Cabinet. The Cabinet has already approved the payment of 50 percent of additional compensation in addition to the assessed value of lands acquired for the above project in installments within a period of four years from 2016-2019.
Grant Assistance from Japan to strengthen public security and prevention of terrorism
The Government of Japan has agreed to provide productions under non-project grants of 250 million Yen (Rs. 327 million approx.) within 2016/2017 to strengthen public security and activities against terrorism.
Accordingly, the proposal made by Prime Minister Ranil Wickremesinghe, in his capacity as the National Policies and Economic Affairs Minister, to sign exchange notes with the Government of Japan to obtain the grants, was approved.
Project for improvement of financial sector to modernise finance field of Sri Lanka
The project to improve the financial sector will implement several essential reforms identified for the development of the field within 2017-2022. It will be implemented under three aspects of financial assistance for reforms, strengthening capacity of regulatory agencies, and development of infrastructure.
Accordingly, the proposal made by Prime Minister Ranil Wickremesinghe, in his capacity as the National Policies and Economic Affairs Minister, to implement the above project by the Finance Minister, the Central Bank of Sri Lanka, Securities and Exchange Commission, and Sri Lanka Insurance Board within 2017-2022, and to obtain a credit of Special Drawing Rights equivalent to USD 75 million from the International Development Agency of the World Bank for the purpose, was approved.
Obtaining assistance of Sri Lanka Scouts Movement in introduction of sustainable solution to conserve natural resources and to build up human-wildlife co-existence
The proposal made by Sustainable Development and Wildlife Minister Gamini Jayawickrama Perera to conduct a training programme for three years for the Sri Lanka Scouts Movement on the purpose with the assistance of Education Ministry at the Randenigala Training Centre of Sri Lanka Wildlife Trust, was approved.
Amendment to Industrial Disputes Act – representation and appearance before Labour Tribunals
It has been identified that some misinterpretations of certain Presidents of Labour Tribunals, preventing representatives other than lawyers to advocate the victims, have caused a prejudice to general employees. Accordingly, the joint proposal made by Labour and Trade Union Relations Minister W.D.J. Seneviratne, and Justice Minister Wijayadasa Rajapakshe to instruct the Legal Draftsman to amend the Industrial Disputes Act making provisions to a person who is not a lawyer to represent or advocate parties, to remove provisions preventing a lawyer from advocating in an inquiry conducted before Commissioner of Labour or authorized officer, and to transfer the power of taking action against employers who neglect orders made by such a tribunal from the Commissioner of Labour to Tribunal Presidents, was approved.
Developing the National Hospital of Sri Lanka into a state of the art modern hospital
It is timely to develop the NHSL to a state of the art hospital which is considered as the apex centre of Sri Lankan health system, which comprises 3,382 beds. Accordingly, the proposal made by Health Nutrition and Indigenous Medicine Minister (Dr.) Rajitha Senarathne to implement following urgent measures as a part of this project, was approved.*Renovating Old Victoria Building and Bandaranaike building
* Renovating quarters of doctors in Barns Place and quarters of Nurses in No. 93.
Establishing a modern Neonatal Specialist Centre at De Soyza Maternity Hospital, Colombo
De Soyza hospital provides healthcare for maternal, obstetrics and Gynaecology, sub-fertility, baby care and family planning and medical care and provides training for undergraduate and postgraduate students of the University of Colombo. Cabinet has already approved the expansion of Premature Baby Unit and Special Care Baby Unit as a large number of births take place in the hospital and many children are transferred from peripheral hospitals.
Accordingly, the proposal made by Health Nutrition and Indigenous Medicine Minister (Dr.) Rajitha Senarathne to establish a Neonatal Specialist Centre in the hospital under assistance of the government of France, was approved.
Draft bill on credit information service providers
Sri Lanka Credit Information Bureau is the sole authorised body to provide credit information in Sri Lanka and the government has identified the positive impact of private credit information service providers on the economy. Accordingly, the proposal made by Finance Minister Ravi Karunanayake to publish the draft bill in the gazette and subsequently present in parliament for approval, which has been drafted to issue license for credit information service providers, and to provide for their regulation and supervision, was approved.
Funding essential infrastructure projects through a government owned Special Infrastructure Company
The proposal made by Finance Minister Ravi Karunanayake to establish the 'Special Infrastructure Company' owned by the Sri Lankan Government and Road Development Authority enabling efficient investment in expressway projects, to transfer assets of current expressways and expressways to be constructed in the future and to earn capital from investors using those assets as a base or to obtain loans by providing them as sureties, was approved.
Allocating a block of land from Ratnapura New Town to construct Ratnapura District Cultural Centre
The proposal made by Megapolis and Western Development Minister Patali Champika Ranawaka to transfer a land of one acre from the Farm Garden Estate owned by the UDA to Ratnapura District Secretary on the purpose, was approved.
Allocating a block of land from Ratnapura New Town to construct Sabaragamuwa Provincial Office of Department of Community Based Correction
The proposal made by Megapolis and Western Development Minister Patali Champika Ranawaka to transfer a land of 20 perches from Farm Garden Estate owned by UDA to the Department of Community Based Correction through Ratnapura Divisional Secretary on the above purpose, was approved.
Allocating a block of land from Ratnapura New Town to construct Ratnapura District Office of Department of National Community Water Supply
The proposal made by Megapolis and Western Development Minister Patali Champika Ranawaka, to transfer a land of 1 rood and 29 perches from the Farm Garden Estate owned by the UDA to the Department of National Community Water Supply through Ratnapura Divisional Secretary on the above purpose, was approved.
Allocating lands to Science, Technology and Research Ministry, University of Sri Jayewardenepura, University of Moratuwa from Mahenawatta of Pitipana, Homagama
UDA has acquired lands of about 34 hectares from the above estate to establish institutions related to science and technology. The proposal made by Megapolis and Western Development Minister Patali Champika Ranawaka to transfer lands of about 46 acres from the above estate to the Science, Technology and Research Ministry, the University of Sri Jayewardenepura, the University of Moratuwa subject to the payment of assessed value of the land, was approved.
Implementating livelihood development and poverty alleviation projects for fisheries community
Funds of Rs. 1200 million have been allocated in 2017 to the Fisheries and Aquatic Resources Development Ministry for the projects. Accordingly, the proposal made by Fisheries and Aquatic Resources Development Minister Mahinda Amaraweera to implement 30 priority projects identified by the ministry with the assistance of the private sector, was approved.
Amendment to Code of Criminal Procedure
(Special Provisions) Act No. 2 of 2013 – (rights of detainees – access to legal counsel)
The Cabinet has already granted approval on 10-01-2017 to make above amendments and the proposal made by Justice Minister Wijeyadasa Rajapakshe to publish the 'Code of Criminal Procedure (Special Provisions) (Amendment) Bill' prepared by the Legal Draftsman and cleared by the Attorney General, in the gazette and subsequently present in Parliament for approval, was approved.
Infrastructure development of construction equipment operator training centre
The centre established in 1982 in Galkulama, Anuradhapura under the Construction Industry Development Authority is a priority institution providing manpower to construction field in Sri Lanka. The proposal made by Housing and Construction Minister Sajith Premadasa to identify necessary infrastructure developments to be done in this centre under a comprehensive plan, was approved.
Signing a statement of mutual agreement between Port of Colombo and Port and Urban Projects Bureau of Kobe City Government
The proposal made by Ports and Shipping Minister Arjuna Ranatunga to sign the statement during the International Port Conference in Kobe, was approved.
Constructing houses for plantation community – Request to increase allocation per house from Rs. 650,000.00 to 1,000,000.00
Houses are being built for plantation community under the Hill Country New Villages, Infrastructure and Community Development Ministry on loan and grant assistance basis. According to estimates prepared in 2014, Rs. 650,000 has been allocated per a house but considering current prices of construction material, it is insufficient. Accordingly, the proposal made by Hill Country New Villages, Infrastructure and Community Development Ministry U. Palani Thigambaram to increase the cost for a house from Rs. 650,000 to Rs. 1,000,000 and to provide Rs. 480,000 of them as an non-repayable grant and the rest Rs. 520,000 as a loan to be repayable in 15 years, and to provide the full amount as a non-repayable grant for those who are being informed to vacate their residences due to landslide threats, was approved.
Establishing National Commission on women
As prevailing institutions on women's affairs has no legal power to address the increasing violence against women, the proposal made by Women and Child Affairs Minister Chandrani Bandara to establish the National Commission on Women, considering the responsibility assigned on the government by the constitution and the Convention on Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination, was approved.
Improving Infrastructure in Saraswathi Central College, Pussellawa
About thousand students study in this school, which is about 100 years old, and it has been identified as a school to be developed with facilities for students of plantation community. Accordingly, the proposal made by Education Minister Akila Viraj Kariyawasam to enter into an agreement with the Republic of India to develop infrastructure of this school under Indian assistance, and to implement the developments by the Education Ministry with the assistance of the Central Provincial Ministry of Education, was approved.
Rehabilitating Moragoda Ela and Temple Bypass
International Development Agency has provided USD 147 million to implement strategic urban development projects in Kandy and Galle. Accordingly, the proposal made by Megapolis and Western Development Minister atali Champika Ranawaka to award the contract for the rehabilitation done by a portion of the grants, to the M/s Business Promoters & Partners Engineering (Pvt.) Ltd at Rs. 844,907,205.00 without VAT as recommended by the Standing Cabinet Appointed Procurement Committee, was approved.
Compensation for victims of boat accident in the seas off Katukurunda, Kalutara on 19-02-2017
The proposal made by Disaster Management Minister Anura Priyadarshana Yapa to pay compensations for victims of the above accident as Rs. 100,000/- for a deceased person and Rs. 20,000/- for an injured person, was approved.
