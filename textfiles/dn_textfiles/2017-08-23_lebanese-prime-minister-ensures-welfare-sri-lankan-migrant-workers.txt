## Title: Lebanese Prime Minister ensures welfare of Sri Lankan migrant workers
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Local
## Date: Wednesday, August 23, 2017 - 01:00

Lebanese Prime Minister ensures welfare of Sri Lankan migrant workers

Lebanese Prime Minister Saad Hariri said the Lebanese Government would facilitate the legalization process of undocumented Sri Lankan migrant workers and ensure their safety and welfare.
Foreign Affairs State Minister Vasantha Senanayake, during his visit to Lebanon, met Lebanese Prime Minister Saad Hariri at his official residence at Grand Serial and discussed on furthering the bilateral diplomatic and labour relations into the new spheres of cooperation in the trade, economic, socio-cultural fields for the mutual benefit.
State Minister appraised the role played by Sri Lankan migrant workers and requested Lebanese Government's assistance to facilitate the legalization of nearly 5,000 undocumented Sri Lankan migrant workers living and working in Lebanon and also to facilitate the repatriation of already registered workers under the Resolution No. of 11360/K.M.Ain/1. He said Sri Lanka wishes to send more skilled workers to Lebanon.
State Minister met General Hassan Ali Ahmad and Captain Assile Hamzeh of General Security of Lebanon and the Lebanon-Sri Lanka Parliamentary Group at the Lebanese Parliament.
Sri Lanka Ambassador to Lebanon H. M. G. R. R. K. Wijeratne Mendis and the Actg. Director General of the Middle East and Africa Division C. A. Chaminda I. Colonne of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs participated.
