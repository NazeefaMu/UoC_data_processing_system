## Title: Case fixed for further trial
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Law & Order
## Date: Tuesday, October 30, 2018 - 01:00

Case fixed for further trial

A case filed against Kandy District parliamentarian Mahindananda Aluthgamage for allegedly purchasing a house worth Rs.27 million at Kynsey Road, Colombo 7 over a complaint that he had allegedly purchased properties through illegally acquired money was yesterday fixed for further trial on November 7 by Colombo High Court.
The case was fixed for further trial since the permanent judge was on official leave.
The Attorney General had filed indictments against Mahindananda Aluthgamage alleging that Aluthgamage had purchased a house at Kynsey Road, Colombo 7 from an individual named Christopher Roshan for Rs.27 million.
The Attorney General alleged that the accused has failed to disclose how he raised the money to purchase the house and further alleged that he had committed an offence that comes under section 3 and 4 of the Prevention of Money Laundering Act. Deputy Solicitor General Thusith Mudalige appeared for the Attorney General. President's Counsel Shavindra Fernando with counsel Kanchana Ratwatte appeared for the accused.
