## Title: [Cabinet Decisions]-04.03.2016
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Political
## Date: Friday, March 4, 2016 - 01:00

[Cabinet Decisions]-04.03.2016

Cabinet Committee on Economic Management
The Cabinet Committee on Economic Management headed by the Prime Minister has been established to make recommendations to the Cabinet of Ministers related to implementation of laws and related subjects related to economic affairs, monetary and financial policy, national investment programme, facilitating private sector investments, investments and economic development of the country. The proposal made by Prime Minister Ranil Wickremesinghe, to introduce a suitable method to make more efficient the implementation of recommendations made by the committee, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
Golden Jubilee of Sri Lanka Tourism
The focused development of Sri Lankan tourism sector commenced with the establishment of the Ceylon Tourist Board in 1966 after the enactment of Tourist Board Act No. 10 of 1966. It has earned high revenue within the recent 50 years, and has generated about 300,000 direct and indirect employment opportunities. Tourist arrivals in 1966 amounting to 19,079 has increased to 1,798,380 in 2015. The importance of promoting and developing the Sri Lankan tourism industry has been emphasised by the economic policy of the government presented to the parliament by the Prime Minister as well as in the Budget Proposal 2016. The Tourism Development and Christian Religious Affairs Ministry has organized commemoration programme to mark the golden jubilee of the Sri Lankan Tourism Industry. Above information presented by Tourism Development and Christian Religious Affairs Minister John Amaratunge, was considered by the Cabinet of Ministers.
Bilateral Air Services Consultations between Sri Lanka and Austria
A discussion to sign a new bilateral aviation agreement between Sri Lanka and Austria has been held with the participation of relevant authorities in November 2015 at Vienna, Austria. The proposal made by Transport and Civil Aviation Minister Nimal Siripala de Silva, to sign the said new agreement by an authorised agent of the government, and to take suitable action for the productive implementation of the agreement, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
Sri Lanka to ratify Ottawa Convention
Northern and Eastern provinces in Sri Lanka have been severely affected by landmines and explosives due to the conflict situation prevailed in Sri Lanka. Demining process is important for the resettlement of internally displaced persons and threats for the lifestyle and infrastructure development in the area by these explosives should be prevented. The joint proposal made by Foreign Affairs Minister Mangala Samaraweera and Prison Reforms, Rehabilitation, Resettlement and Hindu Religious Affairs Minister D.M. Swaminathan, to ratify the anti-personnel mine ban convention alias the Ottawa Convention by the government to make that background, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
Port Access Elevated Highway Project
It has been proposed to build an elevated highway of 5.8 km long at an estimated cost of Rs. 36 billion, from New Kelani Bridge, Peliyagoda to Colombo Fort connecting the Colombo Fort area with the expressway network, for uninterrupted transportation of cargo and containers from Colombo and suburbs to Colombo fort and for reducing the traffic congestion in Colombo urban area. The proposal made by Higher Education and Highways Minister Lakshman Kiriella, to implement the said project and to obtain necessary funds, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
Transport Connectivity and Asset Management Project funded by the World Bank
Many international aiding organisations, including World Bank, Asian Development Bank are following the Output and Performance based Contract method in rehabilitation and maintenance of roads in many countries. The proposal made by Higher Education and Highways Minister Lakshman Kiriella, to implement the rehabilitation of Peliyagoda- Puttalam road as a pilot project under the above method and to discuss with the World Bank to obtain required funds, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
Land blocks from Regional Industrial Estates to setup industries
The Regional Industrial Estates Programme is being implemented by the Industry and Commerce Ministry with the view to promoting industries in the regional level. The proposal made by Industry and Commerce Minister Rishad Bathiudeen, to allocate lands from relevant industrial estates to investors/institutions that have fulfilled required qualifications with the recommendations of the Project Evaluation Committee of the Ministry, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
UDA land to Colombo Plan Secretariat
The Colombo Plan Secretariat which is an international bureau established in Sri Lanka and currently being located in a rented out building has been proposed to be relocated in a premises with sufficient room. The proposal made by Megapolis and Western Development Minister Patali Champika Ranawaka, to provide a land in Perahera Mawatha Colombo 3 on a 50 year lease agreement for the relocation of the above secretariat, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
Supply of fishing equipment to the fishers in Mullaitivu
Fishers in Mullaitivu District have confronted many difficulties with distraction and loss of fisheries equipment due to the 30 year long war. The Indian government has agreed to provide 150 units of fisheries equipment and engines installed externally to fisheries boats on the request of the Fisheries and Aquatic Resources Development Ministry to provide relief to those fishers. The proposal made by Fisheries and Aquatic Resources Development Minister Mahinda Amaraweera, to enter into a Memorandum of Understanding for obtaining of the said equipment valued at Rs. 100 million as a grant from the Indian Government, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
Redevelopment of Bambalapitiya Flats
The Bambalapitiya Flats housing complex in 10 acres and 15.55 perches consists of 259 condominium residential units and 10 condominium commercial units. This building as located near the sea has decayed and has become to a dangerous situation. The proposal made by Housing and Construction Minister Sajith Premadasa, to implement the Redevelopment of Bambalapitiya Flats Project with foreign investor recommended by the Board of Investment, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
Advanced Passenger Information Processing and Passenger Name Record System
The proposal made by Telecommunications and Digital Infrastructure Minister Harin Fernando, to engage Information and Communication Technology Agency and Socie'te' Internationale de Te'le' communications Ae'ronautiques as key stakeholders in establishment of the above system for the Department of Immigration and Emigration, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
Project for Issuing Vehicle Emission Test Certificates
Agreement entered with the two companies that are responsible of establishing island wide emission test centres to issue Vehicle Emission Test Certificates by the Department of Motor Traffic will expire on 01-07-2016. Accordingly, the proposal made by Hon. Nimal Siripala de Silva, Minister of Transport and Civil Aviation, to carry out procurement activities to select few private sector companies as contractors to implement vehicle emission test standards by establishing vehicle emission test centres again for the next 05 years, and to grant permission to establish test centres of the Sri Lanka Transport Board for 09 provinces to test the vehicles owned by the Transport Board, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
Development of Advanced Technological Institutes
It has been planned to establish an Advanced Technological Institute in every district for fulfilling the tasks assigned to the Sri Lanka Institute for Advanced Technological Education (SLIATE) which is functioning under the Higher Education and Highways Ministry. Eleven Advanced Technological Institutes and six Advanced Technological Institute Sections are being implemented under the SLIATE. There is a high recognition for the Higher National Diplomas awarded by the institute. Due to insufficient space available, the number of students enrolled for 2015 was only 35 percent of the more than 20,000 applicants.
The opportunities for the students have been limited to gain advanced technological education in Engineering, Quantity Surveying, English and Information Technology which have a high demand in the labour market. The proposal made by Higher Education and Highways Minister Lakshman Kiriella, to implement projects to develop the capacities of buildings, equipment, staff and students of Advanced Technological Institutes in Kegalle, Dehiwala, Jaffna, Galle, Gampaha, Kandy, Kurunegala, and Sammanthurai, at an estimated cost of Rs. 2,368.5 million, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
Elevated Highway from New Kelani Bridge to Rajagiriya
It has been proposed to construct an elevated highways network in Colombo to reduce the traffic congestion and to build the road connectivity suburbs and expressways. It has been proposed to construct a road of 6.9 km long with four lanes from New Kelani Bridge to Rajagiriya. It will be connected with the Outer Circular Highway in the second phase and the northern end will connect to the Katunayake Expressway. The proposal made by Higher Education and Highways Minister Lakshman Kiriella, to proceed with the project, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.

The proposal made by Health, Nutrition and Indigenous Medicine Minister Rajitha Senaratne, to award the above tender to the bidder recommended by the Cabinet Appointed Standing Procurement Committee for purchase of above vaccines used in children immunisation programme, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.

Signing of SAARC Youth Charter
The above charter aims at promoting health, well being, rights and potential of youth. It also expects to use the knowledge of the South Asian youth in national development and to provide employment opportunities for all utilizing their skills productively. The proposal made by Prime Minister Ranil Wickremesinghe, in his capacity as the National Policies and Economic Affairs Minister, to sign the above charter at the 36th SAARC Council of Ministers to be held in Kathmandu, Nepal on March 17, and to ratify the same, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
Joint Action Programme between Sri Lanka and Thailand to implement MoU on cooperation in the field of tourism
Above Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) was signed between the two countries in 2013. The proposal made by Tourism Development and Christian Religious Affairs Minister John Amaratunge, to sign the joint Action Plan between the Ministry of Tourism Development and Christian Religious Affairs of Sri Lanka and the Ministry of Tourism and Sports of Thailand to implement the MoU during the forthcoming visit of Thailand Deputy Prime Minister in Sri Lanka, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
Paddy Purchasing Programme during the Maha season
Harvesting of paddy in the Maha season 2015/2016 has commenced. The Paddy Marketing Board has planned to purchase paddy in 19 districts according to the government's policy in paddy purchasing. The proposal made by Rural Economic Affairs Minister P. Harrison, to purchase paddy directly from farmers with the cooperation of District Secretaries/Government Agents and to purchase a kilogram of Keeri Samba for Rs. 50, a kilogram of Samba for Rs. 41 and a Kilogram of Nadu for Rs. 38, and to purchase paddy subject to a maximum quantity of 2000kg, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
Vehicles for Government Agencies under the Operating Leasing Scheme
The government spends a considerable amount of money on the purchase and maintenance of vehicles obtained for the use of public sector institutions due to high cost of repairs and the administrative delays.
In order to overcome the weaknesses in the system and to reduce the unnecessary cost incurred on maintaining a large vehicle fleet by each and every public institution under the present system and to enhance the efficiency while promoting the leasing industry in the country, the Budget 2016 proposed to introduce an 'Operating Leasing System' for obtaining vehicles for eligible officials and pool-vehicles for government institutions and state owned enterprises.
A portion of vehicles required for government institutions will be obtained from leasers selected through the government's procurement procedure on "use and return" option and on a 60-month fixed rental basis. Under this scheme, the leaser will bear charges for the insurance, license, all repair costs and the government will only bear the cost for fuel and drivers. The proposal made by Finance Minister Ravi Karunanayake, to implement the 'Operating Leasing System' that saves the maximum cost for the government, was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers.
