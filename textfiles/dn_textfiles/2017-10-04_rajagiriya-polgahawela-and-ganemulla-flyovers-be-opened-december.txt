## Title: Rajagiriya, Polgahawela and Ganemulla flyovers to be opened by December
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Local
## Date: Wednesday, October 4, 2017 - 01:00

Rajagiriya, Polgahawela and Ganemulla flyovers to be opened by December

 The Rajagiriya flyover and two other flyovers, namely, Polgahawela and Ganemulla, which cost over Rs 10,310 million, are scheduled to be opened before the end of this year.
The flyovers to be constructed with intersections, will ease traffic congestion, reduce delays on the road, minimize accidents and lower the operational cost of vehicles and finally, ensure safe and efficient transportation for the people.
Construction of the 542 metre long Rajagiriya flyover, which costs Rs 4700 million, commenced on April 19, this year. Rajagiriya is the main entry point to Colombo city and its suburbs, namely Kollupitiya, Welikada, Nawala and Borella.
The Polgahawela flyover which is also at the final stage of construction will be opened for transpotation in December. Due to the railway station, Polgahawela town experiences heavy traffic congestion and Polgahawela is also the entry point of number of cities.
The 883 metre long Polgahawela, costing Rs 2280 million, will be a great benefit to Polgahawela and Kurunagala (A 19) road users.
Ganemulla is one of the main cities of the Gampaha district. Ganemulla flyover is 504 meters long and it costs Rs 1,830 million.
The construction of the flyovers is funded by UK Steel Bridge project.
