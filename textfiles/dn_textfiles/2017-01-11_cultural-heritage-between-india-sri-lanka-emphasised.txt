## Title: Cultural heritage between India, Sri Lanka emphasised
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Local
## Date: Wednesday, January 11, 2017 - 01:00

Cultural heritage between India, Sri Lanka emphasised

Maha Mahind Vihar in Ulhasnagar Chief Incumbent Ven. Naluwela Ananda highlighted the shared cultural heritage and the linguistic similarities between Sinhala and Sanskrit. The Thera encouraged the exchange of Buddhist scholars between India and Sri Lanka.
The Thera was speaking at ceremony at the temple to commence the work of the Consulate General of Sri Lanka in Mumbai for 2017.
The staff of the Consulate General of Sri Lanka in Mumbai commenced work for the year 2017 by reaffirming the oath of the public service and offering alms to the Buddhist Sangha in Maharashtra.
Maha Mahind Vihar in Ulhasnagar Chief Incumbent Ven. Naluwela Ananda Maha Thera and the Maha Sangha in Maharashtra chanted pirith invoking blessings on Sri Lanka.
Principal Secretary for Minorities Development Department of the Maharashtra Government Shyam Tagade participated in the alms giving with the staff.
