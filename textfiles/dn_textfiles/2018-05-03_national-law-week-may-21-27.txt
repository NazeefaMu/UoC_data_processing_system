## Title: National Law Week from May 21 to 27
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Local
## Date: Thursday, May 3, 2018 - 01:00

National Law Week from May 21 to 27

The Bar Association of Sri Lanka (BASL) in collaboration with the Justice Ministry and the Legal Aid Commission of Sri Lanka, has declared the week from May 21 to 27 as the National Law Week.
The President of the Bar Association of Sri Lanka, Attorney-at-Law U.R. De Silva (PC) said yesterday the National Law Week that will be held under the theme, ‘Legal fraternity towards a law-abiding society’, aims to build a law-abiding society by empowering the people of the country.
Addressing a press briefing held in Colombo yesterday, he said that island-wide legal aid clinics will be held in Colombo, Matale, Jaffna, Galle, Matara, Anuradhapura, Batticaloa, Kurunegala, Vavuniya and Gampaha from May 26 to 27. The public can forward their legal problems through the hotline 0113133872 during the two-day free legal aid programme.
The main legal aid clinic and exhibition will be held at Sri Jayewardenepura Kotte, on May 27 with the participation of Chief Justice, Attorney General, judges, members of the legal fraternity both from the official and unofficial bar, IGP and other dignitaries.
Island-wide legal clinics will also be held at ten centres. Public awareness programmes for teachers, university students, schoolchildren and public as well as the police will also be conducted during the week.
Meanwhile, a census will be conducted on detainees who are unable to pay fines imposed on them by Courts. As a part of the National Law Week, the BASL is to fund selected individuals who are currently imprisoned due to their inability to pay fines.
