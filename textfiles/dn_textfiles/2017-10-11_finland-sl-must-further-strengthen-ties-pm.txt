## Title: Finland, SL must further strengthen ties: PM
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Local
## Date: Wednesday, October 11, 2017 - 01:00

Finland, SL must further strengthen ties: PM

Finland’s Minister of Social Affairs and Health Pirkko Mattila admired Prime Minister Ranil Wickramasinghe’s presence in Finland during the 100th anniversary of its independence in 2017.
She added that they value the relationship with Sri Lanka in the field of democracy, human rights, social justice, education and Health.
Marttila at the Dinner meeting with the Prime Minister on Monday said it is important to strengthen the development activities and entrepreneurs sector between the two countries.
Factors affecting the health sector in both the countries were also discussed.
Prime Minister Ranil Wickremesinghe said that Sri Lanka and Finland has a proud history of providing free education and Health for citizens.
He said that using the experiences in past, it’s time to focus on further strengthening those sectors.
Ports and Shipping Minister Mahinda Samarasinghe, Education Minister Akila Viraj Kariyawasam, Prime Minister Secretary Saman Ekanayake, Finance Ministry Secretary H.A. R Samarasinghe, Sri Lankan Ambassador to Norway Jayantha Palipana, Prime Minister’s additional Secretary Saman Athavudhahetti, Additional Secretary to the Legislative Council Uresha Fernando, Finland Counsel in Sri Lanka Amos Cyril David Gyllenbogel, Sri Lankan Counsel in Finland Ashrof Omar, Chairman of the Sri Lanka – Finland Business Council Jayantha Samarakkoon and Aero International Director Suresh Subramaniyam also participated.
