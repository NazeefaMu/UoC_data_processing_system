## Title: Indian minister Ravi Shankar Prasad calls on President: Pledges cooperation in IT
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Local
## Date: Wednesday, January 17, 2018 - 01:00

Indian minister Ravi Shankar Prasad calls on President: Pledges cooperation in IT

The Indian Union Minister for Law and Justice, Electronics and Information Technology Ravi Shankar Prasad called on President Maithripala Sirisena at the Presidential Secretariat, on Monday.
A member of the Bharatiya Janata Party, Ravi Shankar Prasad is a lawyer representing the State of Bihar as a Member of Parliament and the pioneer of Project Development Fund, which provides electronic services and financial assistance to Indian citizens.
Minister Prasad appreciated the improvement in information and technology of Sri Lanka under the current government and explained the methods of the use of Information Technology in the social development of India.
The President expressed the importance of improving connections between Indian and Sri Lankan tech streams and the Minister promised to provide the maximum support to Sri Lanka.
Minister Ravi Shankar Prasad visited Sri Lanka to deliver the Lakshman Kadirgamar Memorial Lecture. He signed a Memorandum of Understanding in Information Technology between India and Sri Lanka at the Telecommunication and Digital Infrastructure Ministry, yesterday.
Indian High Commissioner to Sri Lanka Taranjit Singh Sandhu was also present on the occasion.
