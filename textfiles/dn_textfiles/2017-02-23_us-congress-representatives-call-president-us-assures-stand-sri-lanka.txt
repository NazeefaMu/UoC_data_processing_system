## Title: US Congress representatives call on President: US assures to stand by Sri Lanka
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Local
## Date: Thursday, February 23, 2017 - 01:00

US Congress representatives call on President: US assures to stand by Sri Lanka

A bi-partisan group of representatives of the United States Congress visiting Sri Lanka lauded the steps taken by the Sri Lankan government for reconciliation, development, good governance and upholding citizen’s rights.
The delegation during a meeting with President Maithripala Sirisena on Wednesday at the Presidential Secretariat, assured that USA will stand by Sri Lanka in its challenging journey towards economic development.
The leader of the House Democracy Partnership (HDP) Group Congressman Peter Roskam expressed admiration on behalf of the US House of Representatives for the transformation process that the President has led after his election two years ago.
The aim of the visit was to promote the on-going partnership between HDP and the Sri Lanka Parliament while strengthening democratic institutions and deepening bilateral relations.
President Sirisena, expressing satisfaction over strong bilateral relations between the two countries, said Sri Lanka is committed to further strengthening relations in the economic and investment spheres.
The delegation accompanied by US Ambassador to Sri Lanka Atul Keshap and Sri Lankan Ambassador to US Prasad Kariyawasam, included Congressman Peter Roskam (Chair – Republican), Congressman David Price (Democrat), Congressman Gerald E. Connolly (Democrat) and Congressman Adrian Smith (Republican).
