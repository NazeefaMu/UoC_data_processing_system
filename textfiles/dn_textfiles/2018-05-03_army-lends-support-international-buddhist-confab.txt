## Title: Army lends support to international Buddhist confab
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Local
## Date: Thursday, May 3, 2018 - 01:00

Army lends support to international Buddhist confab

The Sri Lanka Army lent its support for the conducting of an international Buddhist conference organized by the Collective of World Buddhists, which began in Colombo on May 1.
The conference titled 'Environment-Friendly Sustainable Development' got underway on the evening of May 1, at the Sri Sadaham Sevana premises in Rajagiriya, Borella.
In response to a request for assistance extended to Army Commander Lieutenant General Mahesh Senanayake by Ven. Athuraliye Rathana Thera, Convener, Pivithuru Hetak, the Army assisted the entire project by preparing the landscape, hall arrangements, decorations, preparation of infrastructure facilities and coordination with other agencies, with the support of Sadaham Sevana organizers.
Troops of the Security Force Headquarters - West (SFHQ-West), Quarter Master General of the Army, Directorate of Agriculture, Directorate of Army Band and Drama Art Performance, Corps of Engineer Services (CES) and other auxiliary wings, on the directions of the Army Commander, jointly assisted in the conference arrangements.
President Maithripala Sirisena was the chief guest at the inauguration of the conference. Health, Nutrition and Indigenous Medicine Minister Dr. Rajitha Senaratne was also present. Buddhist representatives from more than 20 nations are attending the conference.
Several international Buddhist organizations, including the China - Sri Lanka Buddhist Association, Shaddha Sevana Foundation, foreign representatives and devotees are taking part in the conference which will go on for four days.
The Army and Air Force Commanders had separate discussions with some of the nominated delegates during their stay at the venue as invitees to the occasion.
The Quarter Master General's office at the Army Headquarters on the instructions of Quarter Master General Major General Bimal Vidanage took the lead in streamlining all arrangements.
A cultural spectacle by Army cultural troupes will add colour to the sessions during the conference.
Troops also supported the construction of a new sanitary complex and a new fence around the premises at the invitation of the organizers.
Similarly, an agricultural model farm, inclusive of crops free from poisonous substances, maintained by the Army troops, is also on display at the Sadaham Sevana premises.
