## Title: Johnston Fernando allowed to go abroad
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Law & Order
## Date: Thursday, August 10, 2017 - 01:00

Johnston Fernando allowed to go abroad

The Colombo Chief Magistrate yesterday temporally removed the travel restriction imposed on former Co-operatives and Internal Trade Minister Johnston Fernando and allowed him to travel to Singapore for seven days.
Filing a motion before Chief Magistrate Lal Ranasinghe Bandara, counsel Niroshan Siriwardena with Indika Giragamage moved Court that their client be permitted to travel Singapore for seven days from August 13 to 20 since the former Minister’s wife is scheduled to travel to the Singapore National University Hospital for medical treatment.
Former Minister’s passport had been impounded regarding five Court cases filed over non-declaration of assets and liabilities.
The Bribery Commission alleged that the former Minister had failed to declare his assets and liabilities for a period of five years since 2010 to 2014.
The Commission to Investigate Allegations of Bribery or Corruption had filed charges against the former Minister in the Colombo Chief Magistrate’s Court under Section 9 of the Declaration of Assets and Liabilities Law No. 1 of 1975.
