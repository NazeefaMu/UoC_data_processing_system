## Title: Baby elephant’s case re-fixed for trial on July 12
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Local
## Date: Saturday, June 17, 2017 - 01:00

Baby elephant’s case re-fixed for trial on July 12

Court permits surety substitution in Dhammaloka Thera’s case:
The Colombo High Court yesterday, permitted the substitution of surety with another in respect of Ven. Uduwe Dhammaloka Thera regarding a case filed against him.
The case is for possessing a baby elephant without a valid licence.Filing a motion Ven Dhammaloka Thera made a request to substitute a surety with another. Dhammaloka Thera was released on two sureties of Rs.10 million each on a previous occasion. High Court Judge Sampath Wijeratne re-fixed the case for trial on July 12.
This is the first time in Sri Lanka that an accused had been indicted in the High Court by Attorney General over an offence of possessing an elephant without a valid licence following the amendments to the Fauna and Flora Protection Ordinance in 2009.
The Attorney General filed an indictment against Ven.Uduwe Dhammaloka Thera on two counts for possessing a baby elephant without a valid licence.
The indictment has been filed over two counts under section 22 (12) of the Fauna and Flora Protection (Amendment) Act, No.22 of 2009 and section 5(3) (a) of Public Property Act. The Attorney General had named 19 persons as witnesses in the case and three documents as production items in the case.
Wildlife Department officials had taken into custody a two and half year-old elephant calf which was found inside the Alan Mathiniyaramaya Temple in Polhengoda on January 28, 2015.
The Attorney General maintained that Dhammaloka Thera had illegally possessed a stolen property, a baby elephant without a valid licence, an offence under the Fauna and Flora Ordinance.
The Attorney General alleged that Ven Dhammaloka had committed this offence on or around January 28, 2015 within the Colombo High Court’s jurisdiction.
