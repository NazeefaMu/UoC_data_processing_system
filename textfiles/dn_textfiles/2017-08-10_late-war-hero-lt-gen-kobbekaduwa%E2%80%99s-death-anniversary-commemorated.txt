## Title: Late War Hero Lt Gen Kobbekaduwa’s death anniversary commemorated
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Local
## Date: Thursday, August 10, 2017 - 01:00

Late War Hero Lt Gen Kobbekaduwa’s death anniversary commemorated

A series of commemorative events on Monday and Tuesday marked the 25th death anniversary of legendary War Hero, Lieutenant General Denzil Kobbekaduwa of Sri Lanka Army, who fell victim to an LTTE landmine on Aug 8, 1992, at Araly Point, Kayts, together with several other Officers and soldiers.
This year’s commemorative ceremonies took place at Denzil Kobbekaduwa statue in Anuradhapura, and at Thanthirimale Rajamaha Viharaya, which reportedly remained close to the heart of late Lieutenant General Denzil Kobbekaduwa. They were participated by Mrs. Laly Kobbekaduwa, family members and late Lieutenant General’s friends and fellow-mates.
Dry rations were distributed to the poor in Thanthirimale village on Monday followed by alms-giving, Bodhi Poojas and Dhamma sermons at the Thanthirimale Raja Maha Vihara premises.
At Anuradhapura, Mrs Laly Kobbekaduwa, Major General Sathyapriya Liyanage, Colonel Commandant, Sri Lanka Armoured Corps (SLAC), Major General Kumudu Perera, Commander, Security Forces - Wanni, Senior Officers, relatives of late Lieutenant General Kobbekaduwa and members of the public joined to lay floral tributes at the statues of late Lieutenant General Kobbekaduwa.
In a separate programme, an alms-giving to 15 bhikkhus took place at the Lankaramaya premises on Tuesday in addition to other arrangements at Kalaththewa, SLAC Army Camp in Anuradhapura.
Lt Gen Denzil Kobbekaduwa, an old Trinitian, joined the Sri Lanka Army on May 25, 1960. After the basic military training at the Army Training centre, he left for Royal Military Academy, Sandhurst, the UK for further training and was commissioned to the Sri Lanka Armoured corps, as a Second Lieutenant on August 3, 1962.
