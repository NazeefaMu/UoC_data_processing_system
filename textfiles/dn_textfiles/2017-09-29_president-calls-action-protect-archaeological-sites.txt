## Title: President calls for action to protect archaeological sites
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Local
## Date: Friday, September 29, 2017 - 01:00

President calls for action to protect archaeological sites

President Maithripala Sirisena said all parties should fulfill their responsibilities for the preservation and conservation of archaeological sites.
He made this remark addressing a ceremony at the Lakshman Kadirgamar Institute in Colombo on Wednesday to hand over the research book on Tampita Vihara in Sri Lanka to the President.
The book was presented to the readers as a result of a research undertaken by the Humanities Faculty of the University of Kelaniya under the funds of the Higher Education for the Twenty First Century (HETC) project. President Sirisena said: "We must safeguard our historical heritage for the future generations and emphasised the need of fulfilling duties towards protecting Buddhist monks at village temples to ensure their sustainable existence.
The book was presented to the President by its author Ganga Rajani Dissanayake.
The President also presented commemorative plaques to the research team. Most Ven. Niyangoda Vijithasiri, Anunayaka Thera of the Malwatte Chapter delivered a special sermon regarding the national importance of protecting the Tampita Vihara.
The Maha Sangha including, Ven. Bengamuwe Dhammadinna Thera, the Chief incumbent of Sri Pada, the Chief Incumbent of the Somawathi Rajamaha Viharaya Ven. Pahamune Sri Sumangala Nayake Thera, Ven. Thrikunamale Ananda Thera,Ven. Padiwela Soratha Thera of the Bingiriya Devagiri Viharaya, professors and lecturers, including Prof. D.M. Semasinghe, the Vice Chancellor of the University of Kelaniya participated.
