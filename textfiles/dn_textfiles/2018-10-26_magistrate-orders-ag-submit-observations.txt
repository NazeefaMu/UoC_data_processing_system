## Title: Magistrate orders AG to submit observations
## Supertitle: Allegations against prosecution counsels in Bond inquiry:
## Section: Law & Order
## Date: Friday, October 26, 2018 - 01:00

Allegations against prosecution counsels in Bond inquiry:
Magistrate orders AG to submit observations

Colombo Fort Magistrate Lanka Jayarathne yesterday ordered the Attorney General’s Department to submit the Attorney General’s observations before the Court in an open letter on the allegations against the prosecution counsels in the bond inquiry.
On a previous occasion, the Magistrate refused to accept the Attorney General’s observations in a confidential letter on the allegations against the prosecution counsels in the bond inquiry.
Senior Deputy Solicitor General Haripriya Jayasundara stated that they will submit the Attorney General’s observation on the next calling date.
President’s Counsel Wasantha Navaratne Bandara appeared on behalf of the Counsel Chandana Perera informed Court that the Attorney General’s Department officials should not be allowed to appear for this matter.
On a later date, the Defence Counsels appeared on behalf of Attorney-at-Law Chandana Perera submitted before the Magistrate that the State Counsel and the Solicitor General demanded Chandana Perera to come to the Attorney General’s Department where he was demanded to give them the account details of the W M Mendis Company.
Attorney-at-Law Kolith Dharmawardane appearing on behalf of the W.M.Mendis Company submitted his written submission before the court.
President’s Counsel Anil Silva appearing on behalf of Arjun Aloysius pleaded that his client be granted bail. PC Anil Silva further stated that Arjun Aloysius’s wife has some medical issues. PC Anil Silva also said that his client Arjun Aloysius’s first child was also suffering from a serious health issue.
Arjun’s wife cannot take care of their three children, PC Silva added. PC Anil Silva also submitted a written bail application before the court.
On a previous occasion, Attorney-at-Law Sajitha Jayawaradane appearing on behalf of the Perpetual Treasuries Private Limited (PTL) Company pleaded permission to shift its office to another place.
After considering all the matters, the Magistrate ordered the owner of the Perpetual Treasuries Private Limited (PTL) to submit all the details regarding the place to be shifted.
The Magistrate also ordered not to harm the external and internal computer system of Perpetual Treasuries Private Limited (PTL).
Meanwhile, Perpetual Treasuries Ltd. beneficiary owner Arjun Aloysius and its CEO Kasun Palisena were also ordered to be re-remanded till November 8 by the Magistrate over the magisterial inquiry in which they were accused of abetting and conspiring with Arjuna Mahendran to allegedly misappropriate public funds worth Rs.688 million during the Bond auction held on February 27, 2015.
Aloysius and Palisena were also re-remanded by the Fort Magistrate till November 8 over the parallel magisterial inquiry conducted relating to the Bond issue on deleting phone call data from Perpetual Treasuries Ltd. voice recording system and fabricating evidence to the Bond Commission.
Senior Deputy Solicitor General Haripriya Jayasundara with Senior State Counsel Lakmini Girihagama appeared for the Attorney General. President’sCounsel Upul Jayasuriya appeared on behalf of the witnesses. President’s Counsel Anil Silva appeared on behalf of Arjun Aloysius and Attorney-at-Law Jeewantha Jayathilake appeared on behalf of Kasun Palisena. President’s Counsel Wasantha Navarathne Bandara with Raneela Senadheera and Chanithi Athauda appeared on behalf of Counsel Chandana Perera.
