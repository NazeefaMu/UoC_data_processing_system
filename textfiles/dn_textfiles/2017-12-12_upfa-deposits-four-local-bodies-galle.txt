## Title: UPFA deposits for four Local Bodies in Galle
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Political
## Date: Tuesday, December 12, 2017 - 01:00

UPFA deposits for four Local Bodies in Galle

United People’s Freedom Alliance (UPFA) yesterday made candidates’ deposits at the Galle District Election Office for four Local Government bodies in the Galle district in connection with the scheduled Local Government elections.
The respective Local Government bodies for which deposits were made are the Ambalangoda and Hikkaduwa Urban Councils, Balapitiya and Neluwa Pradehsiya Sabhas.
Galle district Parliamentarian and UPFA district leader Piyasena Gamage made deposits for the Neluwa Pradeshiya Sabha while Southern Province Chief Minister Shan Wijayalal De Silva deposited requisite securities in respect of Ambalangoda Urban Council. Subsequently Southern Provincial Councilor Amarasiri Kuruwage made deposits for Hikkaduwa Urban Council and for the Balapitiya Pradeshiya SLFP Chief Organiser for Balapitiya electorate P.Dayarathna De Silva made the respective candidates deposits.
Meanwhile, on Friday Southern Provincial minister U.G.D.Ariyathilake who is also the SLFP Chief organiser for the Akmeemana electorate made the corresponding deposits for the Akmeemana Pradeshiya Sabha
As per news electoral system 12 members will be elected following the ward basis and 8 in proportion to the aggregate of total votes received.
Thus the Ambalangoda Urban Council will consist of 20 councilors.
The Hikkaduwa Urban Council which earlier had only 11 members will get 18 councilors with 11 elected and 7 nominated from the common list.
The Balapitiya Pradeshiya Sabha which contained 17 slots earlier will consist of a total of 31 councilors with 19 elected and 12 nominated from the common list.
The total number of members of the Akmeemana Pradeshiya Sabha will increase up to 26 with 16 elected and 10 nominated from the the common list. Accordingly, the Neluwa Pradeshiya will get 16 members which is an increase of 7 members. For the Neluwa Pradeshiya Sabha, 10 members will be elected while 6 will be nominated.
