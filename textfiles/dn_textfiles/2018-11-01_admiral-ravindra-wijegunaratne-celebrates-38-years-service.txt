## Title: Admiral Ravindra Wijegunaratne celebrates 38 years of service
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Local
## Date: Thursday, November 1, 2018 - 01:00

Admiral Ravindra Wijegunaratne celebrates 38 years of service

Chief of Defence Staff Admiral Ravindra C. Wijegunaratne is the longest serving and senior most military officer in the country, having completed 38 years of service today.Admiral Ravindra Wijegunaratne is a product of Royal College, Colombo 7, who excelled in academics as well as extracurricular activities to be one of the most outstanding students in the school.
He joined the navy on November 1, 1980, as an officer cadet of the 9th Intake and later joined the Britannia Royal Naval College (BRNC) at Dartmouth, UK, being the best cadet of the batch. He was the recipient of the Best International Midshipman Award at the BRNC in 1982. He is specialised in Anti-Submarine Warfare and is a qualified diver, paratrooper, and a special forces person.
Admiral Wijegunaratne was instrumental in forming the navy’s Special Forces element known as the Special Boat Squadron (SBS). He led a number of SBS operations behind enemy lines, including the famous ‘Pooneryn Amphibious Operation’ in 1993 and also led SBS teams on training missions with US Navy SEALS and Indian Navy MARCOS.
Admiral Wijegunaratne has been awarded with four distinguished medals for his acts of gallantry, valour and conspicuous bravery in the face of enemy forces, including the Weerodhara Vibushanaya, Rana Wickrama Padakkama and Rana Sura Padakkama.
He is well known as an expert in Asymmetric Naval Warfare and Small Boat Operations. He earned his BSc (Hons.) degree in War Studies from the Karachi University in Pakistan in 1996 and MPhil in Defence and Strategic Studies (First Class pass) from the University of Madras in India, in 2010.
He was an outstanding sportsman and has won Navy Colours for Rugby Football, Sailing, and Rowing. He is a top-marksman and a golfer who introduced the game to the Sri Lanka Navy.
He commanded the Navy Flag Ship SLNS Sayura and held diverse key appointments such as the First Secretary and Defence Adviser of the Sri Lanka High Commission in India, Commandant Naval and Maritime Academy, Flag Officer Commanding Naval Fleet, Director Naval Special Forces, Director Maritime Surveillance and Director Naval Operations when the last four LTTE floating armouries were destroyed in international waters during 2006/2007, Director-General Services; Commander of Southern, Western, Northern, and Eastern Naval Areas; Director-General of the Sri Lanka Coast Guard, and the Chief of Defence Staff.
He was appointed the Navy Commander on July 11, 2015 and appointed as the Chief of Defence Staff on August 22, 2017. As the Commander of the Navy, he raised two battalions of Navy Marines with the support of the US Marines in 2015.
He is married to Yamuna De Saram, who was crowned Miss Sri Lanka in 1989 and also had a successful career as a model and a tele-drama actress. They are blessed with a 25 year-old-son; Satyajith.
