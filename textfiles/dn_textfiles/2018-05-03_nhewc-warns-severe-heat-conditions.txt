## Title: NHEWC warns of severe heat conditions
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Local
## Date: Thursday, May 3, 2018 - 01:00

NHEWC warns of severe heat conditions

The Natural Hazards Early Warning Centre(NHEWC) of the Meteorology Department yesterday, issued a notice requesting the people to be cautious of an adverse heat weather condition that may arise today. They observed that the maximum heat indexes in some areas had already increased.
People in the North, North Central, Uva and North Western province are requested to be watchful as the maximum heat index had increased, the notice said. The maximum heat index is normally computed taking into consideration the temperature and humidity levels in the atmosphere, according to the Meteorology Department.
According to the notice, people living in areas under “Caution” level, would experience fatigue with prolonged exposure and activity. Continuing activity could result in heat cramps, it furthered. People in areas categorized as “Extreme caution,” may experience heat cramps and heat exhaustion. Continuing activity may cause heart strokes, it warned.
Employees at job sites are expected to stay hydrated and take breaks in shady places as often as possible. Residents are requested to check up on the elderly and the sick. People are also advised not to leave small children inside vehicles. It also requested to limit strenuous outdoor activities, find shade and stay hydrated.
