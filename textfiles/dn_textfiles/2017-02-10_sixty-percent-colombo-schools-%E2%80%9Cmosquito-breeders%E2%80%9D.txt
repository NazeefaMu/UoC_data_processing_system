## Title: Sixty percent Colombo schools “mosquito breeders”
## Supertitle: 
## Section: Local
## Date: Friday, February 10, 2017 - 01:00

Sixty percent Colombo schools “mosquito breeders”

Health authorities have discovered mosquito breeding sites in 102 (60 percent) of schools, including some leading schools in the Colombo district. A total of 9,886 dengue cases and 12 deaths have been reported during this year, Health Ministry sources said.
According to sources, there has been an increase in the number of dengue cases reported from nine( the Colombo, Gampaha, Kalutara, Jaffna, Baticaloa, Kalumunai, Galle, Matara and Ratnapura), districts.
“The Health Ministry has discovered that dengue mosquito control activities in institutions are not satisfactory. A dengue prevention programme conducted on February 1, by the Defence Ministry under the direction of President Maithripala Sirisena discovered mosquito breeding sites in 44 schools (26 percent),”sources said.
Health professionals recommend that state institutions should remove mosquito breeding sites located within their premises regularly and appoint a team to make sure there are no dengue mosquito breeding sites within the premises of the institutions. “Discarded containers should be properly disposed of while water containers in use should be cleaned and stored properly,” sources added.
