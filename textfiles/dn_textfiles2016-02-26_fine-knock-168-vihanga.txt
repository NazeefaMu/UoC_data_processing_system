Vihanga Weerakkody who acted as the captain till Thilan Nimesh returned from Bangladesh after the under 19 world cup scored a magnificent 168 runs against Isipathana Vidyalaya, Colombo in their inter school first eleven cricket match played at Colts Cricket Grounds Colombo recently.
His innings lasted 230 minutes and faced 180 balls. He had seventeen hits to the ropes and six over it and associated in a record fourth wicket partnership of 165 runs with Suwath Mendis who contributed 65 runs. However Cambrians were able to beat Pathanians on first innings.
Cambrians commenced meeting each other in 1969 and the Cambrian captain was Nimal Fernando and this is the highest ever score made by a player from either side as they have met annually on 48 occasions.
Vihanga commenced his cricket career from the Prince of Wales college cricket academy and his first coaches were Suminda Peiris and Sumanda Perera and at the age of eleven he was included in the under 13 squad under Kasun Fernando and the coach was Sumith Fernando and in the following year too there was no change in the captaincy and coaching he played in division two
He played for the under 15 division two team in his first year and the skipper was Tharindu Fernando and in the following year Cambrians reached upto pre quarters under Shenal de Silva and Vihanga notched up four consecutive half centuries in the second round. He played for District and Provincial matches.
In his first year in the under 17 division one under Thilan Nimesh could not come up to expectations but under his captaincy in the following year Cambrians reached upto pre quarters and Vihanga scored 370 runs and his coach was Suranga Wijenayake.
He played for the limited over game against Sebs under Ashen Mendis and last year under Jayanga Peiris he scored 515 runs with three fifties. This year in the absence of the regular skipper he led in nine matches with 680 runs under his belt with the top score of 168 versus Isipatana and one fifty. This year he is coached by Kushan Jayawickrema.
